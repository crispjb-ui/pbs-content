The PBM Landscape: What to Know Heading Into the Next 12 Months

Pharmacy is the line item moving fastest on most self-funded plans, and it is one of the clearest places your team can add visible value over the next year. Here is the landscape we are seeing across the PBM contracts we review at Prescription Benefit Solutions, distilled for the conversations you are having with clients.

Three forces define the market today. Consolidation: the largest PBMs now own the mail and specialty pharmacies that fill the claims and the rebate aggregators that negotiate the rebates, so much of what looks like a negotiation is one company moving money between its own divisions. Rebates: the drug in the preferred formulary position is often the one carrying the larger manufacturer rebate, not the lowest net cost to the plan. Transparency: pressure is rising from federal regulators, state legislatures, and ERISA fiduciary litigation, which means asking hard questions of a PBM is now expected, not adversarial.

Four developments worth raising with clients now:

  • GLP-1 spend keeps climbing, and coverage has become a leadership decision about plan sustainability rather than a formulary footnote.
  • Biosimilars are arriving at scale, but rebate structures often keep plans on the higher-priced brand. The savings exist; capturing them takes contract language.
  • Cell and gene therapies with seven-figure price tags are moving from rare to recurring, and many plan documents, stop-loss layers, and PBM contracts were not written for them.
  • Transparent, pass-through PBM models keep gaining ground, giving clients a real alternative for the first time in years.
