## Audience targeting

**Job titles** (focus on plan sponsors who already have a PBM relationship and are looking for ongoing oversight):
- VP of Benefits · Director of Benefits · Senior Benefits Manager
- VP of Total Rewards · Director of Total Rewards
- VP of Finance · CFO

**Industries:** Same self-funded sweet spot as #1 and #2

**Company size:** 1,001 – 25,000 employees (the PBR is the right offering for plans that justify the twice-yearly engagement; larger plans have internal teams, smaller plans typically can't justify the spend)

**Layered targeting (advanced, after 30 days of data):**
- Lookalike audience built from PBS's existing Substack subscriber list (when ConvertKit/Substack integration allows export)
- Retargeting from `/toolkit/contract-review-readiness` and `/toolkit/optimize-vs-go-to-market` page visitors

**Custom question:**
- "How does your plan currently run PBM oversight?" (dropdown)
  - Internal team handles it
  - Broker / consultant runs it (intermittently)
  - We don't have a structured oversight cadence
  - We're considering bringing on a partner
  - Other

## Ad creative

### Image (1200 × 627 px)

**Concept: Calendar-Plus-Audit-Document Photoreal**

> A photorealistic top-down photo, 1200 x 627 pixels. A printed wall calendar shows the year in two-page spread, with two months prominently circled in red marker: "JUNE-JULY" and "NOVEMBER-DECEMBER" — these are the two PBR cadence windows. A red ballpoint pen rests across the calendar.
>
> Beside the calendar, an audit document is partially visible — the top of the page reads "PHARMACY BENEFIT REVIEW · H1 [YEAR]" in clean serif type. Below it, the visible text shows section headers: "Financial Performance," "Contract Compliance," "Clinical Programs," "Member Experience," "Vendor Management," "Strategic Position" — the six categories of the PBR framework.
>
> Above both elements, in larger handwritten black marker, double-underlined: "Twice a year. Six categories. Twenty audit items."
>
> Below, in smaller handwritten red pen: "The PBR PBS runs for clients, formatted as a self-service framework."
>
> Style: realistic product photography, soft natural daylight, subtle paper texture, slightly imperfect handwriting. Colors: warm cream calendar pages, black printed dates, red marker circles and handwriting, off-white audit document with black serif type, dark wood desk surface. No logos, no client names, no specific dates beyond month names.

---

## Why this is #3 in Tier 1

The PBR is a recurring engagement (twice a year, ongoing) but readers downloading this Toolkit may not yet be ready for the recurring engagement — they may still be at the Contract Review stage.

The funnel works:
- Reader downloads PBR Framework
- Reads the framework, sees the 6 categories
- Realizes they don't have the data infrastructure or capacity to run all 20 items themselves
- Email 5 names the PBR as PBS's twice-yearly offering AND names the Contract Review as the entry product
- Reader who's earlier in the journey opts for Contract Review first
- Reader who's already done a Contract Review (or has the data infrastructure) opts directly for ongoing PBR

This ad gets a slightly different audience than #1 — readers who are further along, often with an existing oversight partner whose contract is ending, looking for a structured replacement.

---

## Connection to PBS service ladder

This is the third of three Tier 1 ads that map directly to the three PBS services:

- **Ad #1:** Contract Review Readiness Checklist → leads to Contract Review engagement
- **Ad #2:** Optimize vs. Go-to-Market Decision Framework → reinforces the Contract Review output (and seeds RFP demand for go-to-market path)
- **Ad #3:** Pharmacy Benefit Review (PBR) Framework → leads to ongoing twice-yearly PBR engagement

Together, the three ads run the full PBS engagement journey. After the first 30 days of testing, scale spend on whichever ad produces the strongest CAC-to-LTV ratio.
