# Evergreen Visual Backlog

## Purpose

21 carousel and infographic concepts with full slide-by-slide specs that were originally drafted for specific Tuesday or Thursday slots, then repurposed to their respective Monday Substack deep-dive articles as supporting tables and sections. The slide specs still live in each source week's newsletter file under the `[REPURPOSED]` heading. Because these concepts are pillar-scoped and not tied to a specific topical news beat, they are evergreen and can rotate into any future Tuesday or Thursday slot where:

- The scheduled Tuesday slot's cycle pillar collides with that week's Monday newsletter pillar (anti-cannibalization swap)
- A holiday or low-energy week (Memorial Day, July 4, summer slowdown, year-end) needs a quick-turn evergreen visual
- A Tuesday cycle slot's assigned format (carousel / messy / clean) does not have a natural week-topic fit
- Content fatigue surfaces on a scheduled slot (per the decoder fatigue rule) and we need a substitute
- An automated weekly roundup falls through and we need a same-day Tuesday or Thursday backup

## How to use

1. Match the open slot to the table below by **format class** (Tuesday cycle slot specifies messy / carousel / clean; Thursday is locked to evergreen rotation Slot A messy / Slot B "Same X" carousel / Slot C "5 Questions" carousel) and by **pillar** (cycle assigns a pillar per Tuesday week).
2. Open the source week's newsletter file at the line listed and lift the full slide-by-slide spec, including AI-image-generation prompts and "WHY IT MATTERS" panel copy.
3. Update the cover eyebrow and context labels to the publishing week's framing. Do not reference the original source week to the audience.
4. Schedule like any other Tuesday or Thursday visual. Log performance in `linkedin_performance_tracker.md`.
5. Update the "Status" column in the inventory table below from `available` to `published [date, week-N]` so the backlog stays accurate.

### Entry 37 detail — Medicare GLP-1 "Bridge" Access Gauntlet (news-pegged; source retained)

_Added June 3, 2026. Unlike entries 1-36 (repurposed-from-deep-dive concepts that point to a source week file), this is **news-pegged** with the source retained inline. **Retained source — CMS "Medicare GLP-1 Bridge Prior Authorization Request Form"** (surfaced by David Brennan, MD / @DavidNBrennan on X, Jun 3 2026; also in the Jun 3 roundup):
- **Two paths.** Part D-eligible diagnosis (moderate-severe OSA; MASH with moderate-advanced fibrosis; type 2 diabetes; CV-risk reduction with established CVD) → patient's Part D plan. Weight-loss-only with no eligible diagnosis → the new **GLP-1 Bridge**.
- **Bridge drugs:** Foundayo tablets; Wegovy injection, HD injection, and tablets; Zepbound Kwikpen.
- **Fail-first, hard-coded:** "There MUST be a denied pharmacy claim submitted to the GLP-1 Bridge **BIN 028918 / PCN MEDDGLP1BR** PRIOR to a prescriber submitting a PA request." The denial is a required step, not a risk.
- **12-question gauntlet (Q1-Q12):** Part D-dx acknowledgment; OSA; MASH; T2D; BMI tiers (incl. ICD E66.811/.812/.813); pre-diabetes; prior MI; prior stroke; symptomatic PAD; HFpEF; uncontrolled hypertension; CKD stage 3a+.
- **Submission:** CoverMyMeds or fax.

**Planned post:**
- **Format:** Thursday Slot A messy-infographic decoder (preferred) OR Tuesday carousel / clean infographic walking the gauntlet. Apply the mobile-feed legibility rule (minimal fine print, payload dominates, ≤5 items per view, large high-contrast type, Canva legibility pass).
- **Hook (confrontational, analytical, NOT political):** e.g., *"Medicare just published its GLP-1 access form. In this design a denied claim isn't a risk. It's a required step."*
- **Lead with GLP-1** (PBS's 64K-ceiling topic amplifier). **Proprietary anchor:** "I read prior-auth criteria for a living."
- **Plan-sponsor bridge (REQUIRED — a Medicare topic must connect to the employer buyer):** Medicare's GLP-1 utilization-management playbook is a template self-funded employers can study; the mechanics (fail-first, comorbidity gating, step-by-step PA) are exactly what commercial PBMs run.
