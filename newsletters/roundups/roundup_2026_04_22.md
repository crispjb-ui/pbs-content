## Article Body

Three of the five stories on my desk this week came out of federal courts. That alone tells you something about where PBM reform is actually happening right now. Here is what matters for your plan.

---

### Sixth Circuit Rules ERISA Preempts Tennessee PBM Anti-Steering Laws

*National Law Review / Mintz | April 7, 2026*

In McKee Foods Corp. v. BFP Inc., a three-judge panel affirmed that ERISA preempts two Tennessee statutes designed to stop PBMs from steering patients to affiliated pharmacies. The court held that the laws crossed the line from permissible rate regulation into plan design, distinguishing them from the Arkansas statute the Supreme Court upheld in Rutledge v. PCMA.

I flagged this because it strengthens the hand of self-funded plan sponsors who want to enforce their own network and steering rules without state interference. The court also confirmed that a plan fiduciary has standing to sue a state commissioner under a credible threat of enforcement. If your broker has been warning you that a Tennessee-style law limits your carve-out options, the answer just changed.

---

### Optum Rx and Caremark Closer to FTC Settlement

*Healthcare Dive | April 2026*

The FTC is making "significant progress" in settlement talks with Optum Rx and Caremark in the ongoing insulin pricing case. Express Scripts settled in February and agreed to offer a net-price "Standard Offering" to all plan sponsors, with compliance required by January 1, 2027.

We are watching the structure of the terms, not the headline. The Express Scripts settlement delinks manufacturer payouts from list price and requires member cost-share to be based on net price. If Optum and Caremark sign similar language, the three largest PBMs will be running parallel net-price offerings inside 18 months. That changes the negotiating posture for every renewal in 2027 and 2028, and it changes what a "standard" contract looks like when we review one.

---

### Circuit Split Widens on 340B Contract Pharmacy Laws

*340B Report | April 10 and April 14, 2026*

Two circuits split the same week. The 5th Circuit upheld Mississippi's 340B contract pharmacy access statute on April 10. On April 14, the 4th Circuit overturned a lower court ruling that had upheld Maryland's version of the same law. State 340B rules now vary by circuit, and drugmakers and hospitals are both pressing their positions.

This matters for employer claims because 340B-acquired drugs dispensed through contract pharmacies can land in your specialty spend with no discount passed through. In my experience reviewing contracts, very few plans have language that forces disclosure of 340B-eligible claims. If you operate across multiple states, your exposure now depends on which circuit you sit in, and you should ask your PBM for a 340B claims report for the last plan year.

---

### FDA Approves First Once-Daily Oral GLP-1 for Weight Loss

*FDA | April 1, 2026*

The FDA approved orforglipron (brand name Foundayo), the first oral GLP-1 cleared for chronic weight management that does not require food or water timing restrictions. An oral option removes one of the last sticky barriers to GLP-1 adherence: the injection.

Employers who excluded GLP-1s for weight loss on utilization grounds will see renewed pressure from members now that a pill exists. I would want to see your current PA criteria and your list-price versus net-cost exposure before you get the question at your next benefits committee. Oral formulations historically carry different rebate structures than injectables, which means your PBM's incentive to prefer one over the other is not what it was last quarter.

---

### One More Worth Watching

The DOL comment period on the proposed PBM compensation disclosure rule closed March 31. If finalized as proposed, self-insured plan fiduciaries would be entitled to rebate, spread pricing, and pharmacy payment data for plan years beginning on or after July 1, 2026. We are drafting client letters now for the day the final rule lands.

---

Three of these five stories share one theme. The legal and regulatory scaffolding that has kept PBM economics opaque is being dismantled in pieces, not all at once. The plans that act on each piece as it lands are the ones showing a different cost curve in 2027.
