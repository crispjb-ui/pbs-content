## The strategic frame (why this account exists)

Ginny's personal profile is THE entity. The AEO/person-entity strategy, the follower-to-newsletter funnel, and the amplifier relationships are deliberately concentrated on her. **Brett's account is NOT a second thought-leadership calendar.** Two voices saying the same things dilutes the entity, splits finite production capacity, and creates "who is PBS's expert?" ambiguity.

What PBS is missing is what a credible second human account is uniquely good at:

1. **Engagement velocity** — early substantive comments from a relevant PharmD profile signal quality to the algorithm in the first hour.
2. **Network activation** — Brett's UNC / Ohio State / academic-medical-center / Vizient network is full of pharmacy leaders whose institutions are large self-funded employers (Hospitals & Health Care is already 9-19% of Ginny's post viewers and growing). Academic medical centers are themselves PBS's buyer profile.
3. **Outreach surface** — warming target accounts and broker contacts so PBS is not a cold name when Ginny reaches out.

**One-line summary: Ginny is the voice; Brett is the credible second witness and the network engine.**

## The positioning: pharmacist-operator, in his own lane

- **Ginny's lane:** contracts and claims (what we see in audits).
- **Brett's lane:** health-system pharmacy operations and purchasing — how drug spend, GPOs, and pharmacy economics look from inside the institutions. Complementary, not competing. The Vizient stint is a quiet asset: aggregator economics, admin fees, and channel pricing seen from the buyer-cooperative side.
- **The hospital-as-self-funded-employer angle is his signature intersection:** "If you run pharmacy at a health system, your institution is also a self-funded employer. You are living this system from both sides."
- **The gap closes honestly:** since January he has led growth and marketing for a pharmacy benefits consulting firm. That is the present-tense role on the profile; the 2020-2026 stretch becomes a career-transition story with a clean ending. Use LinkedIn's native Career Break entry with one confident plain sentence.
- **Public framing of the PBS role:** "Marketing & Growth" (or "Head of Content & Research") at Prescription Benefit Solutions. **Never** framed as ghostwriting or orchestrating Ginny's voice; her content is hers.

## The don'ts

- **Don't build a COMPETING content calendar.** *(Amended Jul 10, 2026: a complementary calendar now exists per `brett_content_track.md` — different lane, different days, own voice, hard interlock rules. The original engagement-first priority stands: if hours run short, the first-hour comments and network work outrank his original posts.)*
- **Don't mirror Ginny mechanically.** Same-minute reshares, identical hashtags, comment-on-everything patterns read as coordinated and can suppress both accounts. Vary timing, skip some posts, write in his own voice.
- **Don't touch the adversarial edge in his own posts early on.** Ginny has earned the named-adversary voice with a body of work. Brett's account stays warm, operational, and curious for the first few months; teeth can come later if a lane develops.
- **Don't frame the relationship as "I run her content."** Publicly: growth/marketing at the firm. Period.
- **Don't duplicate the proprietary anchors verbatim.** He can say "at Prescription Benefit Solutions we review hundreds of PBM contracts a year" as a team member, but his posts should lean on HIS unfakeable details (a named health-system scenario, a purchasing-side observation, a residency-era moment), not recycle Ginny's anchor bank.
