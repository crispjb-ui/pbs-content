# Cross-Week Theme Spacing Audit
## Weeks 16-37 (Q2 and Q3 2026)

**Purpose:** Within-week cannibalization is now fixed via the Thursday evergreen rotation. The next layer is cross-week. If two Monday topics hit similar themes too close together, subscribers experience repetition even when the specific content differs. This audit flags that risk across the Q2/Q3 calendar.

**Method:** Group topics by the underlying theme a reader would perceive, not by the literal title. A reader does not distinguish between "Audit Rights" and "Fiduciary Documentation" after two weeks; they feel "PBM accountability." Flag any cluster where three or more adjacent weeks share a perceived theme.

## Flags

### FLAG 1: Renewal preparation cluster is too concentrated in late Q2/early Q3

**Weeks 25, 27, 31, 35, 37** all sit under the subscriber-perceived theme of "preparing for renewal." Even though each has a distinct angle (countdown, evaluation, RFP, negotiation, readiness), five weeks in 13 on the same thematic orbit will compress engagement by Wk 35-37.

**Recommendation:** Consider swapping **Week 31 (PBM RFP)** or **Week 35 (Renewal Negotiation)** to a fresh pillar. Two strong candidates for swap-in:

- **"The Broker-Partner Playbook"** (Broker/Consultant Resources pillar, see broker_partner_recurring_slot.md). Positions brokers as allies with a recurring content slot. High ROI given broker sensitivity is a known constraint.
- **"PBM Data You Are Not Being Shown"** (Transparency pillar). Specific reports the PBM withholds by default, with exact data requests to send.

If neither swap is appealing, at minimum **reorder** so the cluster is broken up: put Rebate Economics (Wk 29) between PBM RFP and Renewal Negotiation, for example.

### FLAG 2: Cost structure cluster runs 4 of 5 weeks in late Q2

**Weeks 28, 29, 30** are back-to-back-to-back on cost structure (Network Design, Rebate Economics, Site-of-Care). **Week 27 (PBM Evaluation)** also leans financial. That is four consecutive weeks where the dominant theme is "where your dollars go."

**Recommendation:** Insert a Clinical Pharmacy or Member Experience week between them. Clinical Program ROI (Wk 32) could pull forward into this cluster. Alternatively, Week 29 (Rebate Economics) could swap with Week 33 (Member Communication) so the financial cluster gets a human-centered break.

### FLAG 3: Prior Authorization (Wk 16) and Fiduciary Responsibility (Wk 19) are only 3 weeks apart

Both land under "PBM accountability" as a reader perceives it. PA discusses auditing the PA program and demanding reporting. Fiduciary discusses auditing the PBM and demanding documentation. A subscriber reading both may feel the newsletter is on a loop.

**Recommendation:** Not severe enough to reorder, but the **Monday article opening hooks must sound meaningfully different**. Week 19 should NOT open with another "I sat with an employer last month..." audit-flavored narrative. Lead Week 19 with an ERISA legal frame, a court ruling, or a specific CFO conversation about accountability pressure. Change the emotional register even though the pillar is related.

### FLAG 4: No clear "cost containment win" week in Q3

Q3 weeks (27-37) are heavy on process and contract language. A reader could finish Q3 with a clearer understanding of what is wrong without seeing a clean case study of what "right" looks like.

**Recommendation:** Consider using Week 35 or Week 37 to include a "This Is What Good Looks Like" case study section (anonymized client). Or make Week 32 (Clinical Program ROI) explicitly a positive example: "Here are three clinical programs that actually delivered ROI for a client, with the math."

### FLAG 5: Drug pipeline and specialty cluster is well-spaced

**Weeks 18, 22, 34** are each about 4 weeks apart. That is healthy spacing for a related theme. No action needed.

## If the calendar is fixed

Then the mitigations are:
1. **Strengthen the Monday opening hooks on adjacent cluster weeks** so they feel tonally different even when thematically related.
2. **Vary the Substack exclusive tool type across the cluster.** If Wk 25-27-31 all use scorecards, the tool itself starts feeling repetitive. Rotate: scorecard, template, checklist, decision tree, contract language library.
3. **Lean harder on the Thursday evergreen rotation.** With the rotation in place, Thursday visuals across a cluster will be on different pillars by design, which partially offsets the Monday cluster fatigue.
