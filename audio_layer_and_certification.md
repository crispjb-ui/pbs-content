# The Audio Layer + Broker Certification — quick-start docs

Two small programs, one file: the near-zero-effort podcast surface (start now) and the broker certification outline (parked until the partner bench justifies it).

## Part 1 — The audio layer (Ginny can start Monday; ~15 min/week)

**What:** Ginny reads the Monday deep dive aloud; Substack turns the publication into a podcast feed distributed to Spotify/Apple automatically. New consumption mode for commuting CFOs, a crawlable transcript surface for AEO, and clip fuel — from content that already exists.

**Setup (once, ~20 min, admin can do most):** Substack publication Settings → enable podcast; upload cover art (Claude/Canva can produce from the Benefit Blind Spots visual system: Primary Blue field, wordmark, "AUDIO EDITION" eyebrow); confirm the feed lists on Spotify/Apple via Substack's built-in distribution.

**Weekly flow:** Ginny records on her phone (voice memo quality is genuinely fine for this format; quiet room, phone at chest height) reading the Monday deep dive with light ad-libs welcome — the ad-libs are the charm. Admin trims top/tail, uploads as the audio attachment to the same Monday post (Substack supports audio-on-post), done. Rules: same publish morning as the deep dive; the sign-off line is standard: *"The written edition, with the worksheet, is at benefitblindspots.substack.com. I read every reply."* No editing beyond trims — polish is the enemy of the cadence.

**Success read (90 days):** Substack reports listen counts per episode; if listens reach ~15% of post views by October, promote the feed properly (LinkedIn mention, email footer line). If nobody listens, stop without ceremony — total sunk cost will have been ~3 hours.

## Part 2 — Broker certification, v1 outline (DO NOT BUILD until 3-5 partners are COLLABORATING+)

**What:** a half-day "Pharmacy Fluency for Benefits Advisors" workshop + completion recognition, delivered to a partner brokerage's producer team. The lunch-and-learn productized; converts partner firms from "refer us" to "trained by us."

**Curriculum outline (4 modules, 45 min each, built almost entirely from existing assets):**
1. **How the money actually moves** — the Dollar Fable as the teaching spine; definitions, spread, rebate flow, channels.
2. **Reading the contract like a pharmacist** — the five decisive sections; live decode of real (public-corpus) clauses; the Contract Language Library as their reference.
3. **The client conversations** — renewal-season timing, the 90-Day Check, the fiduciary reframe, when to bring in an independent bench (the broker-hero version of the sales playbook).
4. **The toolkit workshop** — running the co-branded worksheets with a client; the data request; what a review engagement looks like from the broker's seat.

**Recognition:** a completion certificate + "our team trained with Prescription Benefit Solutions" language they may use; NOT a designation implying credentialing authority (counsel confirms the naming; "certification" may become "workshop" if needed). Materials: the enablement kit + a workbook assembled from existing toolkits. Delivery: Ginny live (in person or Zoom) for the first three; a pharmacist co-teaches from #2 so the format survives her calendar.

**Trigger to build:** the broker KPI at 3-5 COLLABORATING+ (expected ~Q4 2026-Q1 2027). First candidates: GWCU, then Scott Insurance / Sterling Seacrest Pritchard from the target list. SHRM PDC alignment (the Academy application from the blueprint) can make attendance credit-bearing for their HR-credentialed staff — file the application now (admin), design the course when the trigger fires.
