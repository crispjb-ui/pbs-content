### National Health Spending Jumped 7.3% in 2025, and Your Drug Line Led It

*Health Affairs / STAT News | June 24, 2026*

The country spent $5.7 trillion on health care in 2025, a 7.3% jump over 2024, with CMS pointing directly at GLP-1 use as a major driver. Prescription drug spending alone is projected to clear $560 billion in 2026, growing faster than any other category on the ledger.

I lead with this one because it is not abstract. That growth rate is the headwind your renewal is built on, and the pharmacy line is doing most of the pushing. If your GLP-1 spend is not already segmented in your claims data, ask your broker to pull it out before you sit down for renewal. You cannot manage a number you cannot see.

---

### Optum Rx Settles, and the FTC's Insulin Case Against the Big Three Is Done

*FTC / MLex | June 2026*

Optum Rx reached a settlement with the FTC over insulin pricing, making it the last of the three largest PBMs to fold, after Express Scripts in February and Caremark in March. The case targeted the PBMs and their affiliated group purchasing organizations, the rebate-aggregating middle layer with names like Zinc, Ascent, and Emisar that sits between the manufacturer and your plan.

The GPO layer is where this gets quiet. Your PBM may pass through 100% of rebates, and the GPO it owns is under no such obligation. The settlements crack the door on transparency, but they do not rewrite your contract. The protective language still has to be yours. We review hundreds of these a year, and the rebate-definition section is where the money quietly hides.

---

### Florida Subpoenas CVS Caremark Over Steering and Reimbursement

*Healthcare Dive / Becker's | June 23, 2026*

Florida Attorney General James Uthmeier issued a civil investigative demand to CVS Health, probing whether Caremark steers patients to CVS-owned pharmacies, reimburses its own stores more than independents for the same script, and uses audits to claw back payments. CVS has until late July to produce documents.

State AGs are now doing what federal action has been slow to finish. For employers, the value is not the headline, it is the playbook: patient steering and affiliate reimbursement gaps are auditable in your own claims. Brokers and consultants flag these patterns constantly; the regulators are just catching up to what good advisors already check.

---

### Cassidy Releases a Sweeping 340B Reform Draft

*STAT News / Modern Healthcare | June 25, 2026*

Senate HELP Chair Bill Cassidy put out a discussion draft that would let manufacturers deliver 340B pricing through rebates and claims data rather than only upfront discounts, plus add reporting rules and contract-pharmacy limits. It would be the first statutory update to 340B in 15 years.

340B economics sit mostly with hospitals and providers, not plan sponsors directly. But a shift toward claims-data-based rebates changes how drugs move through channels, and channel routing always finds its way into your specialty spend. Worth monitoring, not acting on yet.

---

### One More Worth Watching

Senators are circling a revived $35 insulin cap for the private market and the uninsured. If it moves, it lands on self-funded plans, so I am tracking the language closely.
