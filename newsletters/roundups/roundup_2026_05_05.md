### FDA Proposes Permanently Ending Compounded GLP-1s

*FDA / STAT News | April 30, 2026*

The FDA proposed removing semaglutide, tirzepatide, and liraglutide from the 503B outsourcing facility bulks list. That is the regulatory pathway that allowed compounding pharmacies to produce lower-cost versions of Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, and Zepbound at volume. The agency cited the resolution of prior supply shortages and a growing number of adverse event reports tied to compounded semaglutide. A public comment period runs through June 29, 2026.

If your plan has been covering compounded GLP-1s as a cost-containment measure, this is a deadline, not a distant policy development. Once finalized, that pathway closes and those members shift to brand products. Your broker should be pulling your current compounded GLP-1 utilization data now: how many members are on it, what the brand price looks like at your contracted rate, and whether a clinical management program is in place for the transition. June 29 is not far.

---

### Supreme Court Signals It Will Preserve Skinny Label Generics

*STAT News | April 29, 2026*

Oral arguments in a case about "skinny labeling" did not go the way brand manufacturers hoped. Under skinny labeling, a generic drug can enter the market using a label that carves out patented indications, letting competition proceed on the unpatented uses while the brand retains exclusivity elsewhere. The justices did not appear to question whether the practice itself is valid.

Several high-spend drugs on employer formularies depend on skinny-label generics to hold unit costs down. A decision preserving the practice (expected by late June) removes one litigation threat that manufacturers have used to keep generics off pharmacy shelves. This one is worth watching for your Q4 formulary review.

---

### Specialty Pharmacy Accreditation Has 1,900 Locations. The Money Flows Through Three.

*Drug Channels | May 5, 2026*

Drug Channels presented proprietary analysis from last week's Asembia AXS26 Summit. More than 1,900 pharmacy locations have earned specialty pharmacy accreditation from one of the two major accrediting bodies. The headline number suggests a competitive market. The economics tell a different story: a handful of PBM-affiliated giants control the volume.

We see this in the contracts we review. PBS works through approximately 100 PBM contracts each year, and specialty routing to PBM-owned pharmacies appears in most of them. The accreditation list reads as diverse. The actual spend concentrates. If your plan has not done a specialty carve-out analysis in the last 12 months, this week's data is the reason to ask your broker for one. The question is whether carving specialty to an independent pharmacy improves your unit economics, and in most cases we review, it does.

---

### Medicaid Cuts Are Coming. Watch What They Do to Hospital Pharmacy Relationships.

*KFF Health News | May 5, 2026*

State lawmakers are moving to shore up hospitals already bracing for Medicaid funding cuts under the federal budget reconciliation package. Rural and urban hospital systems are scrambling for alternative funding as services face pressure.

For self-funded plans, this is a downstream story. Hospital financial distress leads to consolidation or closures. Consolidation complicates 340B contract pharmacy relationships and changes where your members can access specialty drugs. The effects show up in claims data, not press releases. Your broker should be tracking hospital network changes in your geography, especially if your plan has members near rural or safety-net facilities.

---

### One More Worth Watching

The PCMA, the PBM trade association, endorsed HHS Secretary Kennedy's call for nationwide adoption of real-time pharmacy benefit tools this week. When an industry's primary lobbying organization endorses a transparency tool, it usually means one of two things: the tool does not actually change their economics, or the regulatory pressure is strong enough that resistance costs more than support. Either way, real-time benefit checks at the pharmacy counter are moving forward. Ask your PBM what they have live today and how members access it.
