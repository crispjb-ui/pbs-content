## Why Podcast Guesting

Podcast guesting is the highest-ROI channel for PBS right now because:

1. **Zero content creation required.** Ginny already knows the material from weekly deep dives.
2. **Highest per-appearance reach.** One episode in front of 5,000 listeners equals months of LinkedIn posting.
3. **Compounds over time.** Episodes stay searchable for years. Each one is an evergreen backlink.
4. **Drives other channels.** Podcast listeners follow on LinkedIn, subscribe on Substack, and contact PBS directly.
5. **Credibility multiplier.** "As heard on [podcast]" builds authority faster than self-published content.

**Q2 target:** 2-3 podcast appearances (up from 1/quarter in Q1)

## Pitching Process

### Weekly Cadence

| Day | Activity | Time |
|-----|----------|------|
| Monday | Research 2-3 target podcasts (check recent episodes for relevance) | 15 min |
| Tuesday | Send 2-3 personalized pitches | 20 min |
| Thursday | Follow up on pitches sent 7+ days ago (one follow-up only) | 10 min |

**Total weekly investment:** ~45 minutes

### How to Personalize Each Pitch

1. Listen to (or skim) a recent episode to understand the host's style and audience
2. Reference a specific episode in your pitch: "I listened to your episode on [topic] and noticed [angle] that connects to what I am seeing in PBM contracts..."
3. Tailor the topic angle to their audience (CFO audience gets the financial angle; HR audience gets the compliance angle; broker audience gets the negotiation angle)

### What to Include in Every Pitch

- [ ] Specific topic with 3-4 bullet points of what you would cover
- [ ] Credential: "I review approximately 100 PBM contracts annually"
- [ ] Social proof: LinkedIn following (1,374), newsletter subscribers (605+), SHRM appearance
- [ ] Why their specific audience would care (not just why the topic is important generally)
- [ ] Clear ask: "Would this be a good fit for [podcast name]?"

### What NOT to Include

- No media kit or long bio (save for when they say yes)
- No multiple topic options in one email (one clear pitch per email)
- No "I would be happy to discuss any topic" (vague pitches get ignored)
- No attachments (links only if needed)

## Podcast Content Repurposing Checklist

One podcast appearance should generate 10+ pieces of content. This checklist ensures nothing is wasted.

### When You Get the Video/Transcript

**Step 1: Clip Identification (30 minutes)**

Watch/read through and flag:
- [ ] The single strongest "I didn't know that" moment (best clip)
- [ ] Any moment with a specific dollar amount or contract pattern named
- [ ] The 60-second segment that best captures Ginny's voice and expertise
- [ ] Any moment the host reacts with surprise or says "wait, really?" (validates the insight)
- [ ] 2-3 additional strong segments (60-90 seconds each)
- [ ] The best one-liner or quotable sentence

**Step 2: Content Production**

From one episode, create:

| # | Content Piece | Platform | Format | Effort |
|---|--------------|----------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Episode release LinkedIn post | LinkedIn Feed (Wed slot) | Text only, hook + key insight + "link in comments" | 15 min |
| 2 | Best clip (60-90 sec) | LinkedIn Feed (Tue or Thu slot) | Native video upload | 30 min (editing) |
| 3 | Second best clip | LinkedIn Feed (following week) | Native video upload | 20 min |
| 4 | Third clip | Substack Note | Video or transcript excerpt | 15 min |
| 5 | 3-5 pull quotes as text posts | LinkedIn Feed (Wed/Fri slots) | Pure text, quote + context | 10 min each |
| 6 | Carousel from key points | LinkedIn Feed (Tue/Thu slot) | 6-7 slide carousel summarizing the interview | 45 min |
| 7 | Newsletter reference | LinkedIn Newsletter (Monday) | "As I discussed on [podcast]..." with key takeaway | 10 min |
| 8 | Substack deep dive expansion | Substack (Monday) | Take one segment and expand into a full analysis | 30 min (content exists, just expand) |
| 9 | Substack Note: episode announcement | Substack Notes (release day) | Short note with link to episode | 5 min |
| 10 | Substack Note: pull quote | Substack Notes (day after) | One strong quote, no link | 5 min |
| 11 | Speaker credibility update | All bios, LinkedIn About, Substack About | Add "As heard on [podcast]" | 10 min (one-time) |
| 12 | Podcast pitching leverage | Future pitch emails | Reference appearance: "I recently discussed [topic] on [podcast]" | 0 min (just update pitch template) |

**Step 3: Distribution Schedule**

Spread the content over 2-3 weeks to maximize reach without flooding:

| Week | Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri |
|------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Release week | Newsletter reference | Best video clip | Text post about episode | Second clip or carousel | Pull quote |
| Week after | Normal deep dive | Carousel from interview | Pull quote #2 | Normal field note | Pull quote #3 |
| Week 3 | Reference in deep dive if topic connects | Third clip (if strong) | Normal post | Normal post | Normal post |

**Step 4: SHRM Episode Specifically**

For the existing SHRM GLP-1 episode:
- [ ] Request the video file and transcript from SHRM
- [ ] Identify the 3 best clips
- [ ] Pull 5 strongest quotes
- [ ] Create carousel: "3 Things I Told SHRM About GLP-1 Coverage"
- [ ] Write LinkedIn post for release/re-promotion
- [ ] Write Substack Note promoting the episode
- [ ] Add "As heard on SHRM" to LinkedIn headline or About section
- [ ] Reference in the Week 18 Field Note ("One Drug Class to Watch: GLP-1s") as supporting content
- [ ] Use in future podcast pitches as social proof

### Key Principles

1. **Video clips outperform on LinkedIn when they show genuine expertise.** A 60-second clip of Ginny explaining something complex in plain language is more credible than any text post.
2. **Never post the full episode on LinkedIn.** Clips drive curiosity. The full episode lives on the podcast platform.
3. **Quote format works:** "On [podcast], I was asked about [topic]. My answer: [one strong sentence]. The full conversation is worth a listen." This drives downloads for the host, which makes them more likely to invite you back.
4. **Every appearance compounds.** Episode 1 makes Episode 2 easier to book. Episode 2's clips make Episode 3 easier to book. The pitching guide's target of 2-3/quarter builds a library that grows the brand for years.
