> **When to publish:** Once the first paid-only post ships (Tuesday May 13 — *"What I'd Ask"* #2). Not before. The Note works better when there's a live paid post to point at than when the paid tier is purely speculative. Publish Tuesday May 13 mid-morning, after the "What I'd Ask" #2 goes live.

> **Format:** Substack Note (the platform's short-form post format). Posted from the Benefit Blind Spots publication account, not Ginny's personal account.

## Anticipated FAQ replies (drafted, paste if asked)

**"Are the Toolkit PDFs going behind the paywall?"**

> No. The printable Toolkit handouts (PA ROI Audit Scorecard, Channel Pricing Audit Worksheet, etc.) stay free. They live in the Monday and Thursday posts where they originally shipped, and they'll continue to ship free in future weeks. They're the lead-magnet layer; they exist to demonstrate the work, not to be gated.

**"Are the older 'What I'd Ask' posts being moved behind paid?"**

> No. The W15 and W17 "What I'd Ask" posts that already shipped stay free, exactly as they were. The paid gate applies going forward — starting with W19 (today's post) and every "What I'd Ask" after it.

**"What if I can't afford the $100?"**

> No problem at all. The free version covers four pieces a week — the Monday deep dive, the Wednesday roundup, the Thursday Field Note, the LinkedIn Newsletter — plus every Toolkit handout. The free version is the publication. The paid tier is an additional layer.

**"How do I become a founding subscriber?"**

> The founding tier is $300/year, capped at 100 subscribers. You can upgrade from the standard tier inside Substack's subscription settings. It's open now; first-come, first-served until 100 is reached.

**"Is there a monthly option?"**

> Not yet. I'm keeping it annual-only at launch to keep the operations simple. If there's clear demand, I'll revisit it in Q3.
