# Wendell Potter Pre-Publish Checklist

## Per-Piece Workflow

Apply this checklist to every piece in the Wendell Potter contributorship roster (Pieces 1-12 and any future additions). Designed for the cadence of a piece every two weeks. Each stage is gating: do not advance to the next stage until the prior stage's checks pass.

**Companion docs:**
- `wendell_potter_contributorship_strategy.md` (strategic spine, voice rules, cascade architecture)
- `wendell_potter_piece_outlines.md` (Pieces 1-12 outlines)
- `wendell_potter_topic_roster_for_review.md` (external proposal to Wendell)
- Per-piece draft files: `wendell_potter_piece_NN_draft.md` (created when each piece enters drafting stage)

---

## Stage 1: Drafting

- [ ] Outline reviewed and locked (from `wendell_potter_piece_outlines.md`)
- [ ] Opening metaphor present and load-bearing (the metaphor IS the setup; no 200-word ramp before it lands)
- [ ] Named adversaries by company (specific names: CVS Caremark, Express Scripts, OptumRx, SaveOnSP, PrudentRx, Variable Copay, Ascent, Emisar, Zinc, Premier, Accredo, Optum Specialty, CVS Specialty as applicable — never just "the Big 3" or "the major PBMs")
- [ ] Proprietary anchor sentence engineered into the structure (one of the 2-3 pull-quote candidates from the outline; load-bearing for cascade callback)
- [ ] Prescriptive ending: named regulator + named action + named instrument (e.g., "FTC final order under Section 5 authority requiring per-NDC spread disclosure")
- [ ] Opinion stated openly as opinion ("In my view," "What I would argue," "My take is") — anchored to expertise at all times, never freestanding hot-take register
- [ ] Length: 800-1,000 words (Wendell's specified range)
- [ ] Closes with a sentence that pushes the reader to act, share, or change their political stance

---

## Stage 2: PBS Humanize Check (the floor)

Per the existing Humanize Check rule in CLAUDE.md:

- [ ] One unfakeable detail per piece (a specific contract clause reference like §5.03, a specific drug name, a dated reference like "last quarter," a scene moment like "the room went quiet")
- [ ] AI-tell blacklist phrases avoided: "Here's the thing," "The truth is," "Most people don't realize," "paradigm," "game-changer," "at the end of the day"
- [ ] No em-dashes or hyphens as sentence separators (per CLAUDE.md style rule; em-dashes are also AI-tells)
- [ ] No fabricated statistics (use "significant," "substantial," "meaningful" where no verified number exists)
- [ ] Broker-sensitivity rule observed: brokers and consultants framed as allies who flag the gaps; PBM is the structural adversary; never imply brokers missed something
- [ ] Anonymized client examples ("a self-funded employer in the Southeast," not "our client X"); no detail that could reverse-engineer the client
- [ ] Pre-publish check: can a reader point to one thing in this piece only PBS could have written?

---

## Stage 3: AI-Detection Pre-Pass (the ceiling, per CLAUDE.md May 13, 2026 rule)

External tooling required. Sign up for **Originality.ai** as primary (~$0.01 per 100 words for detection; humanizer add-on available). Optional cross-check on **GPTZero** ($9.99/mo Plus tier; different methodology).

- [ ] Paste full piece into Originality.ai detection
- [ ] Record AI-likelihood score
- [ ] **Target: <10% AI-likelihood on Originality.ai**
- [ ] **Cross-check: <10% on GPTZero** (run if Originality scores 5-10%, mandatory if Originality scores 10-20%, skip if Originality scores under 5%)

### If detection score >10%, apply revision techniques

Targeting the highest-flagged passages (Originality.ai will highlight them):

- [ ] **Contractions:** Introduce don't, isn't, can't, I'm, you're, that's, won't, didn't throughout. Aim for 8-12 contractions per 900-word piece. AI-generated text tends to expand all contractions; humans don't.
- [ ] **Break perfect parallelism.** Triple parallelism patterns ("The reform is X. The reform is Y. The reform is not Z.") are the strongest AI tell. Reduce to two-beat with broken structure ("X. Y. Not Z.") or convert to a single longer sentence with varied clauses.
- [ ] **Reduce staccato pairs.** Two-sentence patterns like "Three roles. One vendor." are PBS-channel winning shapes but AI-likely on detection tools. Limit to maximum one per piece, used as a load-bearing closing anchor or pull-quote.
- [ ] **Vary burstiness.** Mix 6-word sentence fragments with 30-word complex sentences. Throw in occasional 4-word ("Pick a lane.") and 5-word ("Not both.") fragments.
- [ ] **Add first-person idiomatic asides:** "Honestly," "I get the impulse," "In my experience," "I'll be straight," "Frankly," "Look —" (without the em-dash, just commas)
- [ ] **Replace formal connectives:** "Additionally" → "And"; "Furthermore" → "Plus"; "Moreover" → "Also" or just delete; "Therefore" → "So"
- [ ] **Add parenthetical asides** where they fit naturally (one per ~400 words is plenty)
- [ ] **Vary openers.** Don't start three consecutive paragraphs with "The." Don't start two consecutive sentences with "When." Watch for repetitive sentence-opener patterns.

After revision pass: re-run Originality.ai detection. Iterate until score drops under 10%.

- [ ] Final Originality.ai score recorded in piece draft file footer
- [ ] Final GPTZero score recorded in piece draft file footer (if run)
- [ ] Date of final detection pass recorded

## Stage 4: Fact-Check

- [ ] Every named statistic verified against current authoritative source within the past 30 days
- [ ] Every named entity (regulator, statute, report, company subsidiary) verified against current public record
- [ ] Every state-count, percentage, dollar figure traceable to a citable source
- [ ] PBM subsidiary ownership chains confirmed against current corporate structure (mergers and divestitures change these)
- [ ] No facts older than 18 months without explicit acknowledgment of date in the piece body
- [ ] FTC report titles quoted verbatim from FTC publications, not paraphrased
- [ ] CMS rule citations include exact effective date

---

## Stage 5: Voice Consistency

- [ ] Reads as Ginny Crisp (clinical pharmacist, PBM-specific structural reform, hundreds of PBM contracts a year)
- [ ] Differentiates from Ron Howrigon's macro-health-system-reform voice
- [ ] Differentiates from Wendell Potter's insurance-industry-insider voice
- [ ] Pull-quote sentences scan well in isolation (load-bearing for PBS cascade callback the following week)
- [ ] Opinion + anchor present on every prescriptive sentence (opinion alone = hot take = breaks expert positioning)
- [ ] No partisan framing; reform language stays at policy-proposal altitude
- [ ] No mention of specific PBS clients or identifying client detail

---

## Stage 6: Submission to Wendell

- [ ] Final detection scores documented in the piece draft file footer
- [ ] Final word count recorded
- [ ] Piece submitted to Wendell via the agreed editorial channel
- [ ] Submission note includes detection scores (signals editorial diligence)
- [ ] Confirmation of intended publish date received from Wendell
- [ ] PBS cascade week scheduled (calendar restructure complete for that week — see `pbs_q2_2026_content_calendar.md` and `pbs_q3_2026_content_calendar.md`)

## Stage 7: Post-Publish (PBS Cascade Week W+1)

- [ ] LinkedIn cross-promo post drafted with first-comment Substack link routing to **PBS Substack deep dive** for the cascade week (NOT back to Wendell — the Wendell traffic flow is the prior week's job)
- [ ] Substack Note drafted naming the Wendell piece for PBS subscribers (light teaser; doesn't replace the Monday cascade)
- [ ] Monday newsletter (week of W+1) opens with quotable callback to the Wendell piece, with one of the pull-quote sentences integrated verbatim
- [ ] Monday Substack deep dive (week of W+1) includes mid-article callout box with pull-quote and bridge to the operational angle
- [ ] Wednesday Substack roundup (week of W+1) features the Wendell piece as one of 4-5 curated items
- [ ] Wednesday LinkedIn text post (week of W+1) — if the topic supports a sub-30-word confrontational hook with a named villain, run cascade-topic; otherwise cycle-pillar
- [ ] Cascade-week Plan Sponsor Toolkit handout (from the piece outline's "Cascade-week PBS angle") drafted in `templates/documents/`
- [ ] Friday tease post (week of W+1) drafted for the following Monday's content (not Wendell-topic; standard cycle)
- [ ] Substack analytics monitored for "from HEALTH CARE un-covered" referrals (track conversion)

## Standing Per-Piece File Convention

For each piece in the roster, create a draft file at `wendell_potter_piece_NN_draft.md` once Stage 1 begins. The file holds:

- The full working draft
- Pull-quote candidates (engineered from the draft, ready for PBS cascade callback)
- Fact-check notes for verification before submission
- Detection-score record (Originality.ai + GPTZero scores after each revision pass)
- Submission date and confirmed Wendell publish date

When the piece publishes on Wendell's pub, archive the draft file in place with a "PUBLISHED" header at the top including the live URL. Do not delete; the draft is the record.
