# Engagement Closeout Kit — the Proof Engine

**Created:** July 3, 2026 (world-class-gap follow-up to the strategy audit). PBS runs ~700 engagements a year and has zero case studies and zero live testimonials; the trust layer is aggregate stats only. This kit makes proof capture a standard step of every engagement close, not a favor asked later. Three asks, one moment: **the findings meeting is the peak of client satisfaction; everything here happens at findings + 2 weeks.**

**Feeds:** the `/results` proof page (website plan item ②), the broker enablement kit, the quarterly briefing deck, paid ad AD-5 (held until the proof page exists), and every future proposal.

---

## The motion (add to every engagement's closing checklist)

1. **At the findings meeting:** Ginny (or the engagement lead) notes the win-story facts in the template below while they are fresh. Two minutes, internal only.
2. **Findings + 3 days:** send the thank-you email (template A) — it plants both asks softly.
3. **Findings + 2 weeks** (after the client has acted on a recommendation): send the testimonial ask (template B) OR the referral ask (template C) — **never both in the same email.** Pick by relationship temperature; the other can follow a month later.
4. **File the win story** in `case_studies/` using the template; mark its permission status. Unpermissioned stories still get written (they train the anonymized pattern library); only permissioned ones publish.

## Win-story capture template (internal, 10 minutes)

```
case_studies/YYYY_MM_{slug}.md

Client (internal name only until permissioned):
Profile for anonymization: [industry], [X,000-employee / X,000-life] [self-funded employer / plan]
Engagement type: Contract Review / PBR / RFP / Claims Review
The situation (2-3 sentences, what prompted it):
What we found (the 2-3 specific mechanics, with §-references where real):
Dollar outcome (verified figure only; contracted/identified labeled correctly):
What the client did with it:
The quotable moment (anything said in the findings meeting worth remembering):
Permission status: NONE / ANONYMIZED-OK / NAMED-OK (date, who approved)
```

## Anonymization + compliance rules (load-bearing)

- Publish nothing without explicit client permission, even anonymized. The ask is part of template B.
- Anonymized profile = industry + size band only; never a combination that identifies (a "credit union in Charleston" identifies; a "financial services employer, 2,000 lives" does not).
- Verified numbers only; label contracted vs identified exactly as the 2025-metrics guardrails require; the illustrative-example disclaimer applies when readers might act on the figures.
- Brokers in the story are always the ones who brought PBS in (the hero framing), or unmentioned. Never a story whose subtext is "the broker missed this."

## Template A — thank-you (findings + 3 days, from Ginny)

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Thank you for the time at the findings review. Watching a team engage
with the numbers the way yours did is the best part of this work.

Two small things, no urgency on either:

If the review earns it after you have lived with the findings for a
couple of weeks, I may ask you for a sentence or two about the
experience. And if a peer of yours is staring down a renewal the way
you were, we are always glad to be introduced; feel free to hand them
the worksheets at rxbs.org/toolkit-library even if a conversation
never happens.

We are here through implementation, whichever path you take.

- Ginny
```

## Template B — testimonial ask (findings + 2 weeks)

**Google review link (captured Jul 13, 2026, from the claimed Business Profile):** `https://g.page/r/CW9cjOrlB5_KEBM/review` — offer it as the low-friction alternative in any testimonial ask ("or, if easier, two sentences here: [link]"). A public Google review does double duty: site quote-card material (with permission) AND the public-reviews signal all three AI engines flagged as missing at the July baseline. The QR-code version (same URL) suits printed material: the briefing handout, the Dollar Fable insert, conference collateral.

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Now that the findings have had a couple of weeks to settle: would you
be willing to give us two or three sentences on what the review was
like and what it surfaced? A title and industry attribution is plenty
("VP Benefits, manufacturing employer"); we would only ever use your
name or company with your written OK.

If it is easier, reply to these three prompts and we will draft it
for your approval:
  1. What prompted the review?
  2. What surprised you in the findings?
  3. What would you tell a peer considering the same thing?

Either way, thank you again for the trust.

- Ginny
```

## Template C — referral ask (findings + 2 weeks, alternate)

```
Hi {{first_name}},

A short one. The plans that most need what you just went through
usually hear about it from a peer, not from us. If anyone in your
network is heading into a renewal or has never seen inside their PBM
contract, an introduction to me by email is the whole ask; I will
take it gently from there.

And if it is your broker who should know us, we work behind brokers,
not around them; happy to be introduced on those terms.

- Ginny
```
