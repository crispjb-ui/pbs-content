# LinkedIn Ad Creative Spec — Channel Pricing Audit Worksheet

**Toolkit landing page:** `rxbs.org/toolkit/channel-pricing`
**PDF:** `templates/documents/week_18_channel_pricing_audit_worksheet.pdf`

---

## Why this is the lead Tier 2 ad

W18 Channel Pricing was the canonical PBS breakout: 17K impressions, +22 followers, 4-5 attributed Substack subs from a single Thursday LinkedIn post. The mechanic resonates with self-funded benefits leaders because the audit produces a concrete dollar magnitude (per-channel margin spread) that connects directly to a contract clause.

The Tier 1 ads (Contract Review, Optimize-vs-Market, PBR) are service-aligned — they convert to PBS engagement. The Tier 2 ads are educational lead magnets — they nurture readers into the welcome sequence, where Email 5 names the Contract Review as the natural next step.

## Audience targeting

Same base as Tier 1 ads. Add:

**Member skills (refined):** Pharmacy Benefits · Specialty Pharmacy · PBM (LinkedIn skill) · Drug Pricing

**Custom question on Lead Gen Form:**
- "Does your plan currently audit per-channel pricing?" (dropdown)
  - We have not audited
  - We audit annually
  - We audit quarterly
  - The PBM provides per-channel reporting
  - Other

This question segments leads by audit-maturity. "We have not audited" + the rest of their job title profile = highest-priority lead for follow-up.

## Ad creative

### Image (1200 × 627 px)

**Concept: Three-Pill-Bottle Photoreal (W18 winning canvas)**

> A photorealistic top-down photo, 1200 x 627 pixels. A clean dark wood desk surface fills the frame, slight perspective angle, soft natural daylight from upper-left. Three identical clear plastic prescription pill bottles stand upright in a horizontal row across the middle of the canvas, evenly spaced. Each bottle has a small white printed prescription label.
>
> Reading left to right, the printed labels read:
>   Bottle 1: "RETAIL · $X.XX"
>   Bottle 2: "MAIL · $X.XX"
>   Bottle 3: "SPECIALTY · $X.XX"
>
> The three dollar amounts are different (small on retail, medium on mail, largest on specialty), all in Plex Mono Medium tabular figures.
>
> Above the three bottles, in larger hand-printed black marker handwriting, double-underlined: "Same drug. Three channels. Three prices."
>
> Around the bottles, three handwritten red ballpoint margin notes:
>   - Above Bottle 1, red note: "MAC list — public"
>   - Above Bottle 2, red note: "Margin — private"
>   - Above Bottle 3, red note: "PBM-owned channel — widest spread"
>
> Below all three bottles, in red marker, double-underlined: "The contract decides which margin you see."
>
> Below that, in smaller handwritten red pen: "PBS reviews 100s of PBM contracts a year. The MAC clause is in every contract; the margin clause is in fewer than half."
>
> A black ballpoint pen rests at an angle in the top-right corner. Style: photorealistic product photography, warm natural daylight, slightly imperfect handwriting (Caveat / Permanent Marker / Kalam-style). Colors: dark wood desk, clear plastic bottles, off-white labels, black printed type, red handwritten ballpoint. No logos, no PBM names, no client names.

### Copy

**Headline (≤70 chars):**
`Same drug. Same dose. Three channels. Three different margins.`

**Description (≤200 chars):**
`Free 2-page audit worksheet. The framework PBS uses across ~100 PBM contract reviews annually to surface per-channel pricing gaps. The MAC list is public. The margin is not.`

**CTA button:** `Download`

### Lead Gen Form

**Form headline:** Get the Channel Pricing Audit Worksheet
**Form description:** A 2-page printable framework. Three audit passes. Paste-ready PBM data request. The pattern PBS sees most often: PBM-owned specialty channel margin runs materially wider than the network-membership clause suggests.
**Confirmation URL:** rxbs.org/toolkit/channel-pricing/thank-you

## Budget + metrics

**Initial test budget:** $500 over 10 days
**Pull spend if:** CPL > $60 (lower threshold than Tier 1 — this is a more accessible offer, so cost should be lower)

**Expected performance:** Based on W18 organic breakout (17K impressions free), this ad should clear $35-45 CPL and produce the highest volume of leads of any single ad. Tier 1 ads produce more qualified leads (more directly connected to PBS revenue); this Tier 2 ad produces volume.

## Variant for A/B testing

After 14 days of baseline data, test a Marked-Up Contract Page variant of the same ad:

**Concept: Marked-Up Contract Page (alternate canvas)**

> A photorealistic top-down photo, 1200 x 627 pixels. An open printed PBM contract page rests on a dark wood desk under soft natural daylight. The page is slightly off-white with a faint horizontal section header in bold black serif type at the top: "§ 4.07 PRICING METHODOLOGY · CHANNEL." Below the header, three short clause snippets corresponding to retail, mail, and specialty channels.
>
> Five handwritten red ballpoint annotations are drawn in the margins:
>   - Red circle around the words "channel" in clause 1, with a red note: "Defined where?"
>   - Red circle around clause 2, red note: "MAC same. Margin different."
>   - Red circle around clause 3, red note: "Specialty carve-out is here"
>   - Red note at top: "Three audit passes surface this in half a day"
>   - Red note at bottom: "Free worksheet · 2 pages"
>
> Same overall aesthetic as the Three-Pill-Bottle variant but on a contract-page substrate.

Test the two variants for 14 days. The variant that produces lower CPL becomes the primary; the other becomes the alternate creative for refresh cycles.
