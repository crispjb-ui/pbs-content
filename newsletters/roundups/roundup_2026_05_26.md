### Three Hospital Systems Sue CVS Health Over Alleged $250M 340B Scheme

*Becker's Payer Issues, Healthcare Dive, AJMC, Fierce Healthcare, Modern Healthcare | May 21, 2026*

Mount Sinai Health System, Michigan Medicine (with Edward W. Sparrow Hospital Association), and the University of Kansas Hospital Authority filed separate federal lawsuits against CVS Health, CaremarkPCS, Caremark, CVS Specialty, and WellPartner. The complaints allege that between 2020 and 2025, CVS subsidiaries depressed payment to hospitals for 340B specialty drug claims and pocketed the difference paid by insurers. Alleged losses: Mount Sinai $121 million, Michigan $66 million, Kansas $62 million. The hospitals are represented by Frier Levitt.

340B economics sit primarily with hospitals and providers, not plan sponsors directly, which is why I almost passed on this story at first. The reason I am leading with it is the mechanism. A contracted intermediary holding back savings that should have passed through to a counterparty is the exact contract architecture self-funded plans face on rebates and specialty routing. The discovery in these three cases will pull PBM specialty-channel contract language into the public record at a level of detail plan sponsors have not been able to access through their own audits. Watch the dockets and ask your broker to do the same.

### Pennsylvania Releases Its Act 77 PBM Study

*pa.gov Insurance Department | May 21, 2026*

Under Pennsylvania's Act 77 Pharmacy Benefit Reform Act, the state Insurance Department published a commissioned study of PBM steering, spread pricing, and a proposal to set pharmacy reimbursement at the national average drug acquisition cost (NADAC) plus a $10.49 dispensing fee. Headline findings: spread pricing exists but is "relatively limited and decreasing" under state regulatory pressure. Replacing current reimbursement with NADAC plus $10.49 would redistribute about $14 million annually to independent retail pharmacies, roughly 0.3% of fully-insured drug costs in Pennsylvania.

Self-funded ERISA plans sit outside PA's direct reach, but this is the first state-funded empirical look at what spread pricing has been worth and where it has already compressed. The $14 million figure is small in absolute terms, which is the PCMA-friendly read. The more useful signal for plan sponsors is the second clause: spread pricing is decreasing under regulatory pressure. The extraction lever that is under pressure is moving. The extraction levers that are not, including net cost per script, rebate gross-to-net by therapeutic class, and affiliated specialty pharmacy share, are still in your contract. They are the lines worth pulling before renewal.

### CVS Sues Tennessee to Block the FAIR Rx Act

*Tennessee Lookout, WJHL, WKRN | May 22, 2026*

Hours after Gov. Bill Lee signed the FAIR Rx Act into law, CVS filed suit in the Middle District of Tennessee against the state Board of Pharmacy and Attorney General Jonathan Skrmetti. The law prohibits PBMs from holding pharmacy licenses and requires divestiture by July 1, 2028. CVS argues the statute is unconstitutional, claims it would force the closure of more than 130 Tennessee retail pharmacies, and is expected to cite the federal injunction against the parallel Arkansas law as precedent. A Tennessee state audit referenced in the suit found that PBMs reimbursed affiliated pharmacies at rates up to 160 times higher than non-affiliated pharmacies on identical drugs.

160 times is not a contract negotiation gap. That is the kind of finding that gets pulled into federal discovery and stays there. Whether the law survives the lawsuit is, for plan sponsors, less material than the audit data the litigation will surface. If your specialty fills concentrate at a PBM-affiliated pharmacy, this is the quarter to sort the claim file by dispensing pharmacy ownership and compare per-claim margin against the network clause in your contract. A 160x reimbursement spread in one state is a question your broker should be prepared to answer about yours.

### House Education and Workforce Passes PBM Kickback Prohibition Act 34-0

*Congress.gov, NCPA, PCMA | May 21, 2026*

HR 7895, introduced in March by Rep. Rick Allen (R-Ga.), cleared the House Education and Workforce Committee 34-0. The bill amends ERISA Section 408 to prohibit PBMs from paying referral fees to brokers, consultants, or other intermediaries in exchange for steering an employer health plan to that PBM. Ranking Member Bobby Scott (D-Va.) supported the bill after Allen agreed to broaden the language to close anticipated loopholes.

The bill targets PBMs paying brokers, not brokers themselves. Most of the consultants and brokers we work with at PBS already operate on flat-fee or fully disclosed commission models and have been asking for this distinction to be drawn in public. The bill, if it advances, is clarifying for the market. For plan sponsors, the action item is straightforward: ask your broker how they are compensated on your PBM arrangement and whether any portion comes from the PBM. The good ones will hand you the answer in writing the same day.

### One More Worth Watching

The HHS Office of Inspector General published its Part D vertical integration report on May 20. PCMA highlighted the finding that net drug costs in vertically integrated and non-integrated Part D plans were similar and that PBM-affiliated pharmacies were reimbursed less on average than non-affiliated ones. The Tennessee state audit found the opposite, with affiliated pharmacies paid materially more. The methodology gap between the two studies is the story to track; both will be cited in the CVS Tennessee litigation.
