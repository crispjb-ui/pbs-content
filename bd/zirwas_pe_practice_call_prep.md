# Call Prep — Zirwas / PE-Owned Practice (Thursday discovery call)

_Prospective client + internal champion. Source: X DM (validates the X channel as a lead engine). Created June 2, 2026._

## Who / context
- **Matthew Zirwas, MD** — physician, ~0.1% equity holder in a **350-provider, PE-owned practice** (likely a PE-backed derm/multispecialty platform). **Close with the PE sponsor, CPO, CEO, and board.**
- The practice is **self-insured, administered by Anthem (Ohio BCBS)**. Anthem's pharmacy is **CarelonRx** (formerly IngenioRx), so the PBM is very likely **bundled and spread-priced**, not pass-through.
- Zirwas is the **champion / introducer, not the decision-maker.** The economic buyers are the **CFO / CPO / PE sponsor / board.**

## Objective of the call
This is **discovery + champion activation**, not a close. Win conditions, in order:
1. Confirm fit and the PBM setup (bundled vs. carved out).
2. Map the decision (who signs, renewal timing, what's prompting the look).
3. Secure the **next step**: the PBM contract + a claims file to review (where real savings get proven), and a **warm intro to the economic buyer.**

## The savings thesis (anchor the conversation here)
A self-insured group on a bundled Anthem/CarelonRx arrangement is almost always **spread-priced and rebate-opaque.** Ginny already framed it correctly in the DM: *"most contracts are traditional and not pass-through… some easy wins and others higher disruption but larger savings."* Offer a **menu**: low-disruption quick wins now, optional higher-disruption structural wins for bigger savings later.

**Frame everything in PE terms:** pharmacy savings drop straight to **EBITDA**, and at a PE multiple that compounds into enterprise value. A pharmacy audit is one of the cleanest margin levers in a PE-owned services business, and it doesn't touch clinical operations.

## Tight agenda (~30 min)
1. Rapport + their goals (5 min)
2. Discovery (15 min, questions below)
3. The opportunity in PE/EBITDA terms (5 min)
4. Next step: data request + champion handoff (5 min)

## Discovery questions
- Is pharmacy **bundled with Anthem or carved out**? Who actually administers the Rx benefit?
- Roughly how many **covered lives**, and any sense of **annual pharmacy spend**?
- When does the current arrangement **renew**?
- **Who owns the pharmacy-benefit decision** (CFO, CPO, PE sponsor, benefits committee)?
- What's **prompting the look now** (cost trend, a PE mandate, a specific high-cost drug)?
- Has anyone **independent** ever audited the PBM contract or claims, or only the parties who profit from it?
- Any **high-cost claimants / specialty trend** they're feeling (derm biologics, GLP-1s)?

## Champion activation (the key move)
Zirwas can't sign, so make him effective:
- Ask: *"Could you get me 20 minutes with whoever owns this, the CFO or the PE sponsor, ideally with you on the call?"*
- Hand him a **forwardable paragraph** he can paste to leadership: a self-insured, PE-owned group on a bundled pharmacy contract is almost certainly overpaying on spread and under-managing specialty; an independent review quantifies it at no cost and the savings flow to EBITDA.

## The next-step ask (creates the real value)
- Request the **PBM/Anthem pharmacy contract + a de-identified claims file.** That's where specific, provable savings come from (the $87M-type wins).
- Make it **free, no-obligation,** and easy: offer an NDA and standard secure data handling.
- Note: if Anthem makes claims data hard to get, that limitation is itself a transparency tell worth surfacing to leadership.

## Credibility to deploy lightly
"We review hundreds of PBM contracts a year." If natural, the realized-savings track record ($87M for clients in 2025) as proof, not a pitch. (Results vary by plan.)

- Zirwas is a **provider**; don't get pulled into buy-and-bill / provider-side economics. Keep the lens on their role as a **self-insured employer.**
- **Don't quote specific savings before seeing data.** Speak in typical patterns, then "we'll quantify it from your file."
- **Confirm Ohio is workable** (the state restriction Ginny flagged).
- He asked Ginny to **include his X link** when she shares his analogy, follow through on that, it costs nothing and cements the relationship.
