## Step 2 — Draft to the register (the ten rules, as writing moves)
- **Opener:** the outline's metaphor, in short declarative COMPLETE sentences; land it with the "It is not a metaphor stretched. It is the [contract/org chart/math]." turn where it fits naturally. This is the one place punch belongs.
- **¶2 is always the connective tissue:** one line of callback to the prior chapter + the map anchor (which stream this is).
- **Body: teach generously.** Walk every mechanic step by step in everyday terms ("buying club," "passes through a lot of hands"). Plain teacherly connectives are welcome: "Simply put," "Here is the problem," "For example." Gloss every acronym, role, and venue in stride; name corporate parents at first mention (UnitedHealth's OptumRx, Cigna's Express Scripts, CVS Health's Caremark).
- **The proprietary anchor** in first person, verbatim-style ("In the contracts I review..."), once.
- **Legislative/policy detail belongs here ONCE** (argument pieces carry their own detail; never preview-dump other pieces' detail).
- **The prescription:** plainly, with the named regulator and instrument, explained so a cold staffer follows the reasoning; two honest paths where the outline gives two.
- **Close:** opinion stated openly ("My view is...") in cool language, then ONE flat forward-pointing handoff sentence to the next chapter. No mic drop, no client homework, ever.

## Step 3 — The kill-your-darlings pass (mandatory, the step that makes it work)
Reread the draft and find: (a) the most quotable sentence, (b) any parallel run of 3+ matched clauses, (c) any colon-reveal or negation-reveal flourish. For each: is it TEACHING or PERFORMING? Performing = flatten into example-driven plain prose ("For example, if X... Or if Y... Each reform risks..."). The final Opening cut its own best line; expect to cut yours.

## Step 4 — The two cold reads
1. **Cold staffer:** a Congressional staffer who has read one prior PBM story — does every term self-explain the first time it appears?
2. **The Wendell margin note:** would any phrase draw "What is this? I don't know"? If yes, it is dead or glossed.

## Step 5 — Fact hygiene + flags
No fabricated statistics anywhere. Every external factual claim (a rule's effective date, a state-law count, a named report, an announced price change) gets listed in the header's **Verify before submission** block for the `/verify-fact` pass at submission week. Prefer structural characterization over numbers when a number is not certain. No em-dash separators; spell out Prescription Benefit Solutions if the firm is named.
