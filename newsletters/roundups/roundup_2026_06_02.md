### Eli Lilly Gives 1,000+ 340B Hospitals Five Days to Submit Claims Data

*STAT News, AHA, Modern Healthcare | June 1, 2026*

Eli Lilly notified more than 1,000 hospitals participating in the 340B drug discount program that they have five days to submit pharmacy and medical claims data or lose their 340B pricing. About 2,300 hospitals have already complied with Lilly's January policy. The roughly 1,000 holdouts include many of the larger systems. The American Hospital Association has asked HRSA to intervene and has proposed a neutral clearinghouse as an alternative.

I am watching this for the duplicate-discount question, not the 340B fight itself. Lilly's whole argument is that hospitals are claiming 340B pricing on the front end while plans are also receiving manufacturer rebates on the back end for the same script. If that is true, the duplicate dollars are sitting with the PBM and the hospital, not the plan. The claims data Lilly is demanding is the exact data your contract probably does not require the PBM to share with you on rebate accuracy. Same data, two very different reasons to want it. Worth a question to your broker this quarter about whether your specialty rebate audit picks up dual-dispensed scripts at all.

### Massachusetts AG Sues UnitedHealthcare for $100M in Alleged MassHealth Fraud

*STAT News, Boston Globe, WBUR, Healthcare Dive | May 29-June 1, 2026*

Massachusetts AG Andrea Campbell filed suit in Suffolk Superior Court against UnitedHealthcare, alleging it falsely manipulated the health status of MassHealth seniors enrolled in the Senior Care Options plan to secure higher payments from the state. The complaint estimates the scheme defrauded MassHealth of at least $100 million. The AG describes it as part of a wider "growth-at-all-costs strategy" with understaffed nursing and abandoned quality controls.

This is Medicaid, not commercial, so the direct cost impact to self-funded plans is zero. The reason I flagged it is the upcoding allegation against the same corporate parent that owns OptumRx. Plan sponsors who carve specialty through Optum receive monthly attestations from a vertically integrated entity that a state AG is now formally accusing of manipulating its own data to extract higher payments from a payer. That is a fiduciary question, not a press release. The 2026 renewal cycle is a reasonable place to ask what independent verification you have on the numbers your PBM is reporting up to your plan.

### PCMA Launches Its Largest-Ever Pro-PBM Ad Campaign

*PCMA, Managed Healthcare Executive, Inside Health Policy | June 1, 2026*

The Pharmaceutical Care Management Association rolled out a seven-figure, multi-channel campaign defending the PBM model. The campaign opens with a spot called "Mom's Medicine" running in the DC market, follows with digital, email, streaming, events, and a podcast push, and quietly tests a rebrand from "Pharmacy Benefit Managers" to "Pharmacy Benefit Companies." PCMA calls it the largest ad spend in its history.

The timing matters. Congress's February PBM overhaul required delinking PBM compensation from drug prices in Part D and full 100% rebate pass-through to employer plans. PCMA is positioning for the next round of reform; the February overhaul is already on the books. For plan sponsors, the practical read is short. Anything a PBM tells you next quarter about the "value" they deliver is being shaped, today, by an ad team. The contract is still the only place that value is measurable. The first quarterly report after the new federal rules take effect is the document that actually answers the question PCMA's ads are asking you to take on faith.

### Medicare Will Not Say How Much the $50 GLP-1 Bridge Will Cost

*STAT News, CMS, KFF | June 1, 2026*

The Medicare GLP-1 Bridge program starts July 1, 2026, offering eligible Part D enrollees Wegovy, Zepbound KwikPen, and the Foundayo oral semaglutide for a flat $50 monthly copay through December 2027. Eligibility opens at BMI 27 with a comorbidity or BMI 35 outright. Independent estimates put the federal cost in the billions per year. CMS has been asked for weeks and has not released its own figure.

For commercial self-funded plans the cost-shift question is straightforward. If a 62-year-old member's spouse on Medicare is paying $50 a month for the same Wegovy your plan is paying meaningfully more for net of rebates, the next open enrollment will surface that gap. The pattern is familiar to anyone who watched diabetes scripts shift toward 340B-eligible sites a decade ago. The plan sponsors I would call ahead of July are the ones with GLP-1 already in the top three by spend.

### One More Worth Watching

Drug Channels Institute released its 2026 analysis of the 340B contract pharmacy market on June 2. Headline finding: nearly two-thirds of the contract pharmacy market is now consolidated among the largest chains and PBMs, and growth is slowing as the segment matures. The same three CVS, Walgreens, and Express Scripts entities that hold most of the commercial PBM share also hold most of the 340B contract pharmacy share. That is the same vertical integration story plan sponsors face on specialty, told from the 340B side of the ledger.
