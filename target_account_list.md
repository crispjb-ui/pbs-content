# Target Account List — the Named-Account Layer

**Created:** July 3, 2026 (world-class-gap follow-up to the 6-month strategy audit). Everything inbound so far has been serendipity; this file makes attention deliberate. It is worked inside the Friday sales hour (`email_gated_toolkit/sales_hour_runbook.md`) and feeds the two primary KPIs directly.

**What this is NOT:** a cold-outreach list. PBS does not cold-pitch. The motion is orchestrated warmth: show up where these accounts already are (their people's posts, their downloads, their profile views), put the right asset in front of them, and let the funnel's own mechanics (toolkits, briefings, PARTNER alerts) convert.

---

## Selection criteria

**Employer accounts (target ~50, build to it over Q3):**
- Self-funded, roughly 500-10,000 lives (the SQL scoring sweet spot).
- Renewal-heavy or margin-pressured industries where pharmacy spend is board-visible: health systems and hospitals (the documented growing audience segment), manufacturing, credit unions/banking, municipalities/districts, higher ed.
- A reachable person: a CFO, VP Benefits, or HR Director who is active on LinkedIn OR already in PBS's orbit (follower, commenter, downloader, newsletter sub).
- Regional gravity helps (Southeast/Charleston network) but is not required; the practice is national.

**Broker/consultant firms (target ~20):**
- Independent or regional benefits brokerages WITHOUT a captive pharmacy practice (they have the gap PBS fills; national alphabet houses mostly do not need us).
- Producers or practice leads active on LinkedIn, or firms whose people already save/share PBS Library carousels.
- Firms adjacent to existing wins (GWCU-like credit-union-serving brokers; Hylant-adjacent regional peers who see PBS work up close).

## How accounts get onto the list (sourcing passes, run monthly)

1. **Downloaders:** every toolkit lead's company (the Sheet) — any company matching the criteria gets a row automatically.
2. **Engagers:** commenters and repeat reactors on PBS posts whose title/company fit; the profile-viewer list after breakout posts (201 viewers followed the anniversary post; nobody harvested them).
3. **Audience segments:** LinkedIn post analytics name the companies viewing (the 10,001+ employee and Hospitals & Health Care segments); pull recurring company names.
4. **Event registrants:** the quarterly briefing registration list (`quarterly_briefing_event.md`) once live.
5. **Ginny's market knowledge:** brokers she respects, employers she has quoted-but-not-closed, conference contacts. (This pass only she can do; seed it in one sitting.)

## The weekly motion (5-10 minutes inside the sales hour, after the hot-lead pass)

Pick **3 accounts** (rotate; prioritize WARM): for each, do ONE deliberate thing:
- comment substantively on their person's post (expertise, never pitch), or
- send the ONE toolkit that matches their situation with a two-line personal note, or
- invite them to the quarterly briefing, or
- for broker rows: the `broker_partner_track.md` motion (that file owns broker execution; this list just feeds it).
Log the touch in the row. An account that engages back twice or downloads = move to WARM; a WARM account with a real conversation = hand to the lead Sheet and work it as a lead.

## Status ladder

`COLD` (listed, no interaction) → `AWARE` (they've engaged with content) → `WARM` (2+ engagements or a download) → `IN CONVERSATION` (handed to the lead Sheet / sales hour proper) → `CLIENT/PARTNER`.

## Guardrails

- Never let the list turn touches into pitches; the touch is value or nothing.
- Broker rows follow the broker-as-hero rules in `broker_partner_track.md` without exception.
- No scraping or purchased lists; every row earned its place through fit + a real signal.
- Review quarterly: prune accounts with 2 quarters of zero response; the list stays ~50/20, not 500.
