## §1 — Internal attestation walk (Ginny signs before /standards publishes)

Walk every PBS revenue stream against each criterion. The page's load-bearing claim is "We accept no PBM revenue and hold ourselves to every standard above." A yes to ANY probing question below means the page language must be adjusted before publish, not explained after.

**Criterion 1 — No PBM revenue.**
- Does Prescription Benefit Solutions receive any payment, in any form, from any PBM: consulting fees, data-access fees, audit-settlement participation, implementation allowances, conference sponsorships?
- Any rebate-aggregation participation or per-claim economics anywhere in any client arrangement?

**Criterion 2 — No ownership entanglement.**
- Any ownership interest (either direction) involving a brokerage, carrier, PBM, or benefits vendor? Any family-member ownership that a journalist could characterize as entanglement?

**Criterion 3 — No contingent placement income.**
- Is any PBS fee in any engagement contingent on which PBM/carrier/vendor the client selects? Any success fees, savings-share arrangements with vendors, or referral fees from copay-program, alternative-funding, or specialty vendors PBS recommends or implements in client work? (The copay-program work PBS performs for clients is the edge case counsel would probe: PBS may be paid by the PLAN for that work; it may not be paid by the program vendor.)

**Criterion 4 — Disclosed compensation, in writing.**
- Does every current engagement letter state PBS's compensation and its source before work begins? If any legacy engagement does not, is that fixed before this page publishes?

**Criterion 5 — Findings owned by the plan.**
- Has PBS ever withheld or filtered findings at a third party's request (broker, carrier, vendor)? Is the standing practice that complete findings go to the plan sponsor?

**Privacy-policy companion attestation (same signature, replaces counsel review of the policy):**
- The PHI paragraph is operationally true: rxbs.org collects only business contact information; no claims data, member data, or PHI moves through the website or its forms. Client claims data travels only through the separate secure channels the policy references.
- The address that publishes in the Contact section is the business address of record, never the home address.

**Sign-off:** date + "I attest each answer above is accurate and the published language is true as written." Retain this signed copy in the fiduciary-file records. Re-walk annually each January alongside the Standards revision, and immediately upon any new revenue arrangement.

## §2 — Client-facing attestation template (the Part 3.4 deliverable)

> **Independence Attestation — Prescription Benefit Solutions**
>
> Provided to: [CLIENT / PLAN SPONSOR NAME] · Engagement: [ENGAGEMENT NAME] · Date: [DATE]
>
> Prescription Benefit Solutions attests, for this engagement and as a standing matter:
>
> 1. We accept no compensation, directly or indirectly, from any pharmacy benefit manager: no consulting fees, no data fees, no rebate-aggregation participation, no implementation allowances.
> 2. We are not owned by, and do not own, any brokerage, carrier, PBM, or vendor whose economics are affected by this review's findings.
> 3. Our compensation for this engagement does not depend on which PBM, carrier, or vendor your plan selects.
> 4. Our complete compensation for this engagement, and its source, is disclosed in our engagement agreement.
> 5. Our complete findings, including anything unfavorable to any incumbent vendor or advisor, are delivered to you without filtering by any third party.
>
> These are the five criteria of The Standards for Independent PBM Review (www.rxbs.org/standards), which we publish and hold ourselves to.
>
> _______________________________
> Ginny Crisp, PharmD · Chief Executive Officer · Prescription Benefit Solutions
