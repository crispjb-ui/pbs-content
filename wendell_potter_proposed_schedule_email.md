It's prescriptive (names regulators and named actions) rather than explainer-mode, so it fits the column's voice. It also gives readers a single piece to anchor the subsequent column to: each piece that follows argues one of the five reforms in detail. Below is the topic plan I've drafted for the early run. I'm happy to share updated topic schedules a few weeks in advance as we work through these, so your editor always has runway on what's coming.

**Opening Piece · Wed Jun 3 · PBM Economics 101: The Five Revenue Streams That Run Through Every PBM Contract — and the Five Reforms Each One Requires.** Most reform debates fix on one revenue stream — spread pricing, rebates, owned-pharmacy margin — and watch the margin migrate to one of the others. The structural picture is unified: five revenue streams, one entity collecting on all five, uneven disclosure floors on each. This piece is the map. Pieces that follow argue each reform.

**Piece 1 · Wed Jun 17 · Spread Pricing: Why the FTC's Next Move Should Be Mandatory Disclosure.** Same drug, same dose, two prices, and the difference vanishes into PBM accounts the plan never sees. The FTC already has the data; this piece names the regulatory move that should follow.

**Piece 2 · Wed Jul 1 · Vertical Integration in PBMs.** Your PBM owns your specialty pharmacy, your insurer owns your PBM, and your PBM writes the rule that decides which pharmacy fills your member's high-cost drug. Three jobs, one vendor, and the bill that should break it up.

**Piece 3 · Wed Jul 15 · GLP-1 Coverage and the PBM Profit Layer.** The GLP-1 access debate everyone is having is the wrong debate. The list-price-to-net-cost gap on the highest-leverage drug class in modern memory is where the money is actually moving.

**Piece 4 · Wed Jul 29 · The Conflict of Interest at the Heart of Copay Accumulator Programs.** Manufacturer copay cards are a legitimate patient affordability tool and plan-sponsor accumulator programs can be a legitimate cost-containment lever, but the same PBM should not be controlling formulary placement, specialty pharmacy routing, and accumulator crediting math simultaneously. The reform is the structural firewall and member disclosure, not the prohibition.

**Piece 5 · Wed Aug 12 · White-Bagging and Channel Steering.** Same drug. Same dose. Same patient. Three different prices, all set by the same PBM, who is paid more when the pharmacy it chooses is its own.

**Piece 6 · Wed Aug 26 · The FTC PBM Report: What Should Happen Next.** In July 2024, the FTC published an interim staff report titled "Pharmacy Benefit Managers: The Powerful Middlemen Inflating Drug Costs and Squeezing Main Street Pharmacies." Nearly two years later there has been no final report and no comprehensive enforcement action; here are five named actions that should follow.

**Piece 7 · Wed Sep 9 · Rebate Aggregators: The Offshore Middlemen.** Ascent, Emisar, Zinc, and Premier are four PBM-owned entities most plan sponsors have never heard of, and they intercept manufacturer rebate flow before the pass-through math is calculated. A structural deception in plain sight.

**Piece 8 · Wed Sep 23 · DIR Fees and Their Successors.** The 2024 CMS rule banned retroactive DIR clawbacks in Medicare Part D, a real win. The PBMs are now reconstructing the same economic effect under new names; this piece names what to watch for.

**Piece 9 · Wed Oct 7 · Specialty Drug Routing Economics.** Beyond white-bagging, specialty routing is where vertical integration generates the largest hidden margin in the modern PBM contract. The PBM-owned specialty pharmacy does not compete on price; it competes on routing.

**Piece 10 · Wed Oct 21 · Manufacturer Patient Assistance Diversion.** A manufacturer foundation pays $5,000 to help a patient afford a specialty drug, and the PBM-owned specialty pharmacy is the gatekeeper, the formulary writer, and the entity that decides whether the assistance counts toward the deductible. Three conflicts, same vendor.

**Piece 11 · Wed Nov 4 · The PBM RFP Methodology Problem.** The PBMs know which scoring methodology your broker is using, and the headline pricing in their proposal is engineered to win that exact methodology. The information asymmetry is structural, and the fix is fiduciary clarity.

**Piece 12 · Wed Nov 18 · The Audit Rights Gap.** Plan sponsor audit rights in PBM contracts are technically present and operationally hollow: the windows are tight, the designees are restricted, the data extracts are PBM-defined. A federal audit-rights floor is the cleanest structural reform on the table.
