# Substack Welcome Email Sequence
## Benefit Blind Spots Onboarding

**Purpose:** Convert passive Substack subscribers into engaged readers and PBS leads. Three-email sequence triggered by the Substack subscribe action.

**Why this matters:** Substack subs inflected from 8 (Mar 7) to 34 (Apr 17). Each new sub currently gets nothing after signup. A welcome sequence that establishes voice, delivers immediate value, and sets cadence expectations is the highest-leverage conversion lever we have not yet pulled.

**Setup:** Substack supports a single welcome email natively. For a multi-step sequence, use Substack's automation (if enabled) or send the second and third emails manually with a subscriber segment. The three emails below are written so Email 1 can stand alone if the sequence gets collapsed to one.

---

## Email 2: Sent 3 Days After Subscribe

**Subject:** Why I started writing this

**Body:**

I did not plan to write a newsletter.

I started PBS because I watched too many self-funded employers get blindsided by their PBM contracts. The language was intentionally hard to read. The reports were designed to look good, not to be useful. The annual review was built around the PBM's calendar, not the plan sponsor's.

We built the audit methodology we use with clients specifically because we got tired of seeing the same patterns repeat: audit rights that did not actually allow audits, rebate guarantees with definitions that gutted them, performance reporting that summarized everything except the line items a CFO would want.

Benefit Blind Spots exists because I kept answering the same questions. A newsletter makes that scalable.

What you will not find here: PBM bashing for its own sake. Fabricated statistics. Generic "transparency" advocacy without specifics.

What you will find: contract language you can actually redline. Scoring rubrics you can apply to your own data. Specific questions your broker can use to push back. And occasionally, a post that makes your PBM account executive uncomfortable.

The goal is a plan sponsor who walks into renewal season with their own numbers and their own priorities, not reacting to what the PBM is offering.

If that is useful, share this with one person in your network who would benefit.

Ginny

## Email 3: Sent 7 Days After Subscribe

**Subject:** Three questions to ask your PBM this quarter

**Body:**

The easiest way to tell whether this newsletter is going to be useful to you is to put one of its ideas to work right now.

Three questions you can send to your PBM account manager today. Use any or all. The answers will tell you more about your plan's position than your last three quarterly reports.

**1. Can you provide a net cost comparison for every therapeutic category where a biosimilar is available?**

This single request reveals whether your formulary is optimized for your plan's cost or for your PBM's rebate revenue. If they cannot produce it, that is the answer.

**2. What is our administrative PA review fee, our clinical pharmacist review fee, and how many of each type processed in the last 12 months?**

Across contracts we review, admin PA review runs $25 to $40 per PA and clinical pharmacist review runs $60 to $80 per PA. Some PBMs bundle these into a flat admin fee so they are invisible. If yours is invisible, ask for itemization.

**3. What is our first-pass approval rate on prior authorizations for maintenance medications in stable patients?**

Anything above 90% means the PA is rubber-stamping. You are paying for friction that is not changing outcomes.

If any of these questions produce a meaningful answer, forward it to me. If any produce pushback, that is also useful. Either way, it is a data point about where your contract actually stands.

Next Monday's deep dive will build on this. You will get it at 7:30 AM ET.

Ginny

*Illustrative example for educational purposes. Actual amounts vary by plan.*

## Deployment Notes

- **Tone check:** Conversational, first-person, no sales pitch. Email 1 establishes voice. Email 2 establishes motivation. Email 3 proves value through immediate actionable content.
- **Trigger sequence:** Email 1 at subscribe. Email 2 at +3 days. Email 3 at +7 days.
- **Reply tracking:** Email 1 and Email 3 both explicitly invite replies. Track reply volume and content as qualitative signal for who is actually engaging vs. passively subscribing.
- **Update cadence:** Revise Email 3's specific questions quarterly based on what topics are performing and what current contract patterns we are seeing.
- **Lead attribution:** If a subscriber replies to Email 1 or Email 3 and a PBS sales conversation results, note the source in CRM. After 6 months, evaluate whether welcome-sequence-sourced leads convert at different rates than other channels.
