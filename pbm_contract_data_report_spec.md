## The load-bearing guardrail (brand rule)
**Qualifiers, not invented percentages.** Per `substack_what_were_seeing.md` writing guidelines + the CLAUDE.md no-fabricated-statistics rule: "a majority of contracts," "roughly half," "nearly every contract we reviewed" — NOT "73%." Specific percentages imply a formal study and would be fabricated. The credibility comes from the volume ("hundreds a year") + the specificity of the *pattern*, not invented precision. **To publish real qualifier-grade numbers, PBS must tally patterns as it reviews** (see "Operational input" below).

## Report structure
1. **Title + thesis** — e.g., *"What We See in Hundreds of PBM Contracts: The 2026 Pattern Report."* One-line thesis: the same handful of structural gaps recur in nearly every contract.
2. **Methodology box** — "aggregated from hundreds of PBM contract reviews; no client identified; patterns are general market observations." (Mirrors the quarterly disclaimer.)
3. **The pattern sections** (one per provision family — the spine of the report; each: the pattern, a qualifier on how common, why it matters, what to ask for):
   - Pricing guarantees (GER / effective-rate true-up direction + timing)
   - Rebate definitions + pass-through (the noun trick; aggregator carve-outs)
   - Manufacturer payments outside the rebate definition (admin/price-protection/placement/data/GPO fees)
   - Audit rights (who picks auditor/records/findings)
   - Termination & transition (auto-renewal, run-out fees, data/rebate-accrual return)
   - Specialty routing / channel steering (own-pharmacy share)
   - Formulary control (rebate-driven tier placement; exclusions)
   - Reporting completeness (the missing line items)
4. **"What this means for plan sponsors"** — the action layer (ties to toolkits).
5. **"What we're watching"** — forward look (ties to the next report + the shocking-fact pipeline).
6. **CTA** — gated download + the audit offer.

## Operational input (so the numbers are real, not fabricated)
Keep a lightweight **review tally**: as PBS reviews each contract, log yes/no flags per pattern (e.g., "one-directional true-up: Y/N," "auto-renewal >90 days: Y/N," "rebate aggregator carve-out present: Y/N"). The report's qualifiers ("a majority," "roughly half") then reflect actual counts, satisfying the no-fabrication rule. A simple spreadsheet with one row per reviewed contract and one column per pattern is enough.
