Five stories this week. Three of them involve the same underlying problem: your PBM knows something your contract doesn't require them to tell you. That is the thread worth pulling.

### The DOL Wants PBMs to Show Their Math. The PBMs Are Saying No.

*PCMA / Department of Labor | April 15, 2026*

The comment period on the Department of Labor's proposed PBM fee disclosure rule closed April 15. The rule would require PBMs to disclose all compensation arrangements to plan fiduciaries under ERISA, including fees paid by third parties. The Pharmaceutical Care Management Association filed formal comments asking DOL to rescind it, calling it redundant of the CAA 2026 PBM reforms already signed into law.

Here is what PCMA did not say: what those fees actually are. The argument that this rule is "duplicative" of existing law reveals exactly how much the industry is counting on the gaps in that law. If you are a self-funded plan sponsor, this rule would give your fiduciaries visibility into every dollar your PBM is receiving, from whom, and for what. Watch whether DOL holds firm or folds under industry pressure.

---

### 4th Circuit Sides With Drug Manufacturers on 340B State Law

*340B Report | April 14, 2026*

A 4th Circuit panel ruled 2-1 to vacate a lower court decision that had upheld Maryland's 340B contract pharmacy access law, again siding with drug manufacturers over the state. This is the second time the 4th Circuit has come down on the manufacturers' side. The 5th Circuit upheld Mississippi's contract pharmacy law earlier this month, creating a clear split between federal circuits.

We pull 340B claims in our contract reviews, and the gap between what covered entities pay for drugs and what they bill commercial plans is often substantial. As this legal question splits across circuits, how your plan's specialty claims flow through hospital-affiliated pharmacies is worth auditing now. Waiting for courts or Congress to resolve the question is a strategy, but not a free one.

---

### The PBM-Insurer Integration Map Just Got Updated

*Drug Channels Institute | April 14, 2026*

Drug Channels Institute released its annual 2026 vertical integration map, tracking ownership relationships among the largest insurers, PBMs, GPOs, and specialty pharmacies. GLP-1 medications drove roughly 60% of retail pharmacy revenue growth over the past five years. And while some consolidation is unwinding, Cigna's Evernorth Health Services invested $3.5 billion for a stake in Shields Health Solutions, a hospital-focused specialty pharmacy.

I flag this report every year because the ownership structures matter for contract language. When your PBM holds a substantial stake in the specialty pharmacy filling your members' highest-cost prescriptions, questions about network exclusivity, site-of-care steering, and rebate allocation become much sharper. Most PBM contracts we review were written before the current ownership chart existed.

---

### FDA Approved an Oral GLP-1. Then Asked for More Safety Data.

*CNBC / BioPharma Dive | April 14, 2026*

The FDA approved Eli Lilly's orforglipron (Foundayo) on April 1, the first oral GLP-1 for weight management. Within two weeks, the agency sent Lilly a request for additional data on liver injury and unexpected cardiovascular events flagged in post-approval review. Post-marketing requirements include outcomes trials for cardiac risk and delayed gastric emptying.

An oral GLP-1 removes the injection barrier, which means the eligible population for weight management coverage expands substantially. Pair that with a pending FDA safety signal, and this is a watch-and-wait situation for employer formularies. If your PBM is already recommending Foundayo for 2027 formulary placement, ask what the post-marketing trial timeline looks like before that decision is made.

### One More Worth Watching

The Senate HELP Committee held a hearing April 16 titled "Making Medicines More Affordable: How Competition Can Lower Drug Prices," with witnesses including critics of the 340B program. Congress returned from recess with a drug pricing agenda that includes reconciliation bill provisions targeting spread pricing in Medicaid and flat-fee PBM structures in Medicare Part D. The momentum for PBM and drug pricing reform is building. Some of what passes will reach employer plans.
