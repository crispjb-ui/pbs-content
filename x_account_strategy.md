## Why X, and what job it does

X is the only new social channel where PBS's proven reach amplifier actually lives most actively. The single biggest documented reach lever in the LinkedIn performance tracker is **Mark Cuban amplification (+60-75% on PBM/340B content)** — and Cuban is far more active and far more repost-prone on X than on LinkedIn. The entire PBM-transparency ecosystem is louder on X than anywhere else: Cuban, Antonio Ciaccia / 46brooklyn, Adam Fein / Drug Channels, Cost Plus Drugs, FTC commissioners, drug-pricing researchers, and the health-policy press.

**What X is for:** reach, amplification, and journalist/policy relationships that feed back into LinkedIn authority.

**What X is NOT for:** direct newsletter/Substack conversion. PBS's buyers (CFOs, HR Directors) make pharmacy-benefit decisions on LinkedIn and in their inbox, not on X. **Treat conversion as near-zero by design.** Measuring X by newsletter subs will wrongly conclude it failed. Measure it by reach, amplification events (Cuban/Ciaccia/Fein reposts), and policy-conversation presence.

**Effort budget:** low, by rule. X works for PBS *only* as a repurpose-and-engage channel. The moment it requires bespoke daily content creation, the opportunity cost against the already-full LinkedIn + Substack cadence makes it not worth it. Cross-post existing content + 5-10 minutes/day of engagement. Nothing more.

**Threads:** when a LinkedIn decoder or dollar-comparison post has multiple beats, post it as a thread (hook post + reply chain). Threads travel further on X than single posts.

## Posting cadence

Start small and sustainable. Scale only if it's working and effort stays low.

- **Phase 1 (first 4 weeks):** 3-5 posts/week. Cross-post Wednesday text post + 2-3 of the week's Substack Notes + react to any breaking PBM news.
- **Phase 2 (if Phase 1 holds):** 1 post/day + news reactions as they happen.
- **News reactions are additive,** not scheduled — fire them when a story breaks regardless of the day's plan. These are the amplification engine.

## Hashtag and format norms (different from LinkedIn)

- **Hashtags: 0-1 max, often zero.** X is not LinkedIn. The LinkedIn 3-hashtag rule does NOT apply. Hashtags do little for X distribution and can read as spammy. Use one topical tag (`#PBM`, `#340B`, `#DrugPricing`) only when it genuinely aids a search/topic conversation, otherwise none.
- **Tagging beats hashtagging.** @-mentioning a journalist, a source, or an amplifier on a relevant post does more for reach than any hashtag.
- **Brevity wins.** X rewards the hook harder than LinkedIn. Lead with the confrontation; cut the wind-up.
- **Links suppress reach on X.** Put links in a reply to your own post, not in the main post (mirrors the LinkedIn first-comment cross-promo discipline).

## What NOT to do

- Don't bespoke-create for X. Repurpose + react only. (The moment it's a content-creation burden, it fails the low-effort test.)
- Don't chase X conversion metrics. It's an amplification channel.
- Don't post politics or polarizing non-PBM content. Same guardrails as the LinkedIn personal-post rule: family/career/PBM-policy only.
- Don't auto-cross-post identically from LinkedIn via a tool that mangles formatting. Light manual touch keeps it native.

## 30-day launch sequence

1. **Day 1:** Claim the handle, set display name / bio / location / link / photo / header. Post and pin the authority post.
2. **Days 1-3:** Follow the Tier A and Tier B patrol lists. Reply substantively to 3-5 amplifier posts to seed presence before posting volume.
3. **Week 1:** Cross-post the current week's Wednesday text post and 2-3 Substack Notes. React to any breaking PBM news same-day.
4. **Weeks 2-4:** Hold 3-5 posts/week + daily 5-10 min patrol. Log follower count and any amplification events weekly.
5. **End of Day 30:** Assess. If you've had even one Cuban/Ciaccia/journalist repost, the channel is earning its (low) keep. If reach is flat AND effort crept above 10 min/day, pause and reassess rather than escalating.
