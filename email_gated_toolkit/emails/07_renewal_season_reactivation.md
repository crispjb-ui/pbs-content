This email reactivates the entire captured list at the moment of maximum relevance.

`Your January 1 renewal is decided in the next 60 days`

## Body

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Earlier this year you downloaded the {{first_toolkit_name}} from
Prescription Benefit Solutions. I am writing now because the window it
was built for is open.

If your pharmacy benefit renews January 1, the decisions are being made
now. Renewal terms land this month or next. Amendment and termination
notice windows, usually 90 days, close in early October. Whatever gets
signed this fall is the contract you live with all of next year.

Three things worth doing in the next 60 days, in order:

(1) Pull the renewal terms next to the current contract and check what
    changed. The changes are rarely announced; they are redlined.
    The worksheet you downloaded is built for exactly this pass.

(2) Send your broker or PBM the data request before you agree to
    anything. Every toolkit's page 2 has the paste-ready version.
    You cannot evaluate a renewal you have no data on.

(3) If the numbers or the language raise questions you cannot get
    answered, get an independent read before the notice window closes,
    not after. A renewal-season second opinion is a fast engagement;
    we run them all fall.

If that third one would be useful for your plan, tell us where things
stand and we will find a time for a short call:
    rxbs.org/request-a-call?topic=renewal-second-opinion

A few sentences on your situation is enough. Or just reply to this
email; either reaches us and we respond within one business day.

Either way: do not sign the renewal unread. That one sentence is most
of what we do for a living.

- Ginny

Ginny Crisp, PharmD | Chief Executive Officer
team@rxbs.org | www.rxbs.org
```
