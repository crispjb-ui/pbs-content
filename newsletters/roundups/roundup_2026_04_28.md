### CMS Extended the GLP-1 Bridge Through 2027. Part D Sponsors Voted With Their Feet.

*CMS / KFF | April 21, 2026*

CMS announced on April 21 that the Medicare GLP-1 Bridge, covering Wegovy and Zepbound for eligible Part D beneficiaries, has been extended through December 31, 2027. The BALANCE Model, which would have brought permanent GLP-1 weight management coverage into Medicare Part D starting January 1, 2027, was pulled from the CY 2027 bid cycle entirely. The stated reason: Part D Sponsors asked for more time and data. The actual reason is the number buried in the announcement. The 80 percent participation threshold was not met.

The plans with the most sophisticated GLP-1 utilization data, the most cost-containment infrastructure, and the most leverage with manufacturers declined to commit for another year. When that segment of the market walks away from a CMS-subsidized model, it is the strongest external signal commercial self-funded plans are going to get that the economics are not yet sustainable at scale. You now have 12 months to let Medicare generate real utilization data before locking a 2027 coverage decision. When your broker brings GLP-1 coverage to the table at fall renewal, bring this data to that conversation.

### PBM-Owned Specialty Pharmacies Are Steering Your Members. New Data Shows the Profits Follow.

*Drug Channels | April 28, 2026*

Drug Channels published its April 2026 roundup Monday with two findings worth flagging for plan sponsors: PBM-affiliated specialty pharmacies capture a disproportionate share of referrals, and vertically integrated Part D businesses show evidence of profit shifting in how MLR is calculated. Both patterns are visible in publicly reported data from integrated PBM/insurer entities.

What shows up in Part D data appears in commercial plans, too. When your PBM steers your members to its own specialty pharmacy, it collects dispensing fees, manufacturer fees, and spread on the same transaction. We flag this pattern in the contract audits we run, and the gap between in-network specialty costs and carve-out market rates can be substantial. Ask your broker to pull a specialty pharmacy network penetration report before your next renewal. If your PBM controls where the scripts are filled, it is controlling a line in your budget you may not be able to see clearly.

### CAA 2026 Mandates 100% Rebate Pass-Through. Your Existing Contract Does Not.

*Consolidated Appropriations Act / Mintz / Groom Law | February-April 2026*

The Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2026, signed February 3, requires PBMs to pass 100% of all manufacturer rebates, fees, and other remuneration directly to the group health plan on a quarterly basis, effective for plan years beginning January 1, 2029. Detailed semiannual reporting on gross and net drug spend, spread pricing, and all forms of PBM compensation are required. Civil penalties of $10,000 per day apply for non-compliance. The concurrent DOL proposed rule adds pre-contract disclosure requirements and plan-selected annual audit rights for self-insured plans.

These provisions do not take effect until 2029. The contract you sign at your next renewal will run straight through that date. If your current PBM contract has no rebate pass-through language and no audit rights, you have a 12 to 18 month window to renegotiate before the law forces the issue. The standard contract we see in our work does not include the protections the CAA will mandate. The law is not a floor that PBMs were already meeting. It is a ceiling they have been operating below.

### FDA Approved an Oral GLP-1 With No Food Restrictions. $149 a Month Cash.

*Eli Lilly / FDA / Pharmacy Times | April 1-23, 2026*

The FDA approved Eli Lilly's orforglipron (Foundayo) on April 1 as the first once-daily oral GLP-1 receptor agonist that can be taken without food or water restrictions. Phase 3 ACHIEVE-4 trial data published April 23 showed 7.5% to 11.2% weight loss at 72 weeks versus 2.1% for placebo. Lilly is pricing Foundayo at $149 per month cash via LillyDirect, with eligible commercial patients paying as little as $25 per month through a savings card.

Oral semaglutide (Rybelsus) required a 30-minute fasting window, which kept uptake low. Foundayo removes that barrier. If your formulary covers GLP-1 injectables, expect member requests for this drug within 90 days. The commercial list price is not yet public, but your net cost will depend on the rebate tier your PBM negotiates, and your prior authorization criteria likely covers injectables only. Plans that did not update formulary language after Rybelsus approved are about to find the gap.

### One More Worth Watching

HRSA closed the comment period on its 340B Rebate Model RFI on April 20, receiving 5,576 responses. The agency is weighing a shift from upfront 340B discounts to a rebate-based model, which would delay cash flow to covered entities but not eliminate the program economics. For self-funded employers, the 340B story that moves your budget is operational: whether your PBM can identify and flag 340B-acquired claims in your data. If they cannot, your plan may be paying full commercial reimbursement on drugs a covered entity purchased at a steep discount. The spread lives somewhere. Ask where.

---

The BALANCE delay and the CAA rebate pass-through story connect. Both are telling you the same thing: the information your PBM holds about your drug spend is the asset, and the gap between what they are required to disclose now versus what they will be required to disclose in 2029 is where your plan is losing money. The time to close that gap is before renewal, not after.

If any of these developments affect your plan, the time to discuss with your broker is now, not at renewal.
