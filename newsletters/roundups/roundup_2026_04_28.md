### CMS Extended the GLP-1 Bridge Through 2027. BALANCE Is Delayed. 80% of Part D Sponsors Said No.

*CMS / Reed Smith / KFF | April 21, 2026*

CMS announced on April 21 that the Medicare GLP-1 Bridge, originally a short-term program covering Wegovy and Zepbound for eligible Medicare Part D beneficiaries from July 1 through December 31, 2026, has been extended through December 31, 2027. The BALANCE Model, which would have brought permanent GLP-1 coverage for weight management into Medicare Part D starting January 1, 2027, has been pushed out of the CY 2027 bid cycle entirely. The stated reason: "CMS received feedback from Part D Sponsors that an extension of the Medicare GLP-1 Bridge would offer a valuable opportunity to provide plans with additional data and facilitate a smoother transition." The unstated reason is the number buried in the announcement. The 80 percent Part D Sponsor participation threshold was not met.

The plans with the most sophisticated GLP-1 utilization data, the most cost-containment infrastructure, and the most leverage with manufacturers declined to participate in a CMS-subsidized coverage model with manufacturer-funded lifestyle support wrappers. When that segment of the market walks away for another year, it is the strongest signal commercial self-funded plans are going to get that the GLP-1 coverage economics are not yet sustainable at scale. It does not change where coverage is ultimately headed. It changes the timing. Plans now have a full year to let Medicare generate utilization and cost data before committing to 2027 terms. If your broker is pushing you to lock in a GLP-1 coverage decision for 2027 renewal, the Part D Sponsors just gave you permission to wait for more evidence.
