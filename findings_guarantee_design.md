# The Findings Guarantee — design document (decision-ready)

**Status:** design complete; gated on (1) the analyst data pull specified below, (2) counsel review, (3) Ginny's go/no-go (Decision #14, after #5's data). Nothing publishes from this file until all three clear.

**The idea:** with 132 pharmacy benefit reviews averaging $469K in identified savings per client, Prescription Benefit Solutions can plausibly offer what no conflicted competitor dares: a fee-backed promise that the review will find real money. Risk reversal converts skeptical CFOs instantly, and the offer itself advertises the hit rate. The design question is structure, not courage.

## 1. The analyst data pull (commission first; ~1 day)

From the 2024-2025 engagement records, for Pharmacy Benefit Reviews and Contract Reviews separately, by client size band (<500 / 500-2,499 / 2,500-9,999 / 10,000+ lives):

1. Distribution of **identified** savings per engagement: minimum, 10th percentile, quartiles, median (not just the mean, which one whale can carry).
2. Percentage of engagements where identified savings were below (a) 1x, (b) 3x, (c) 5x the engagement fee.
3. Any zero-findings or near-zero engagements: how many, and what characterized them (plan size, recent RFP, unusually clean contract).
4. Same cuts for the Renewal Second Opinion scope if separable (renewal-term findings only).

**The decision rule:** the guarantee is offerable at a given threshold if fewer than ~5% of historical engagements in the covered band would have triggered it. If the 10th-percentile engagement still identifies 3x fees, a 2x-fee guarantee is nearly free to offer. If the distribution has a fat tail of low-finding engagements, Option C below (or no guarantee) is the answer. **No fabricated confidence: the pull decides.**

## 2. Three structures (choose after the data)

**Option A — the full waiver (boldest, best marketing):**
> If our review does not identify savings opportunities of at least [2x] our fee, the fee is waived.
Clean, quotable, maximum conversion. Highest exposure; requires the cleanest distribution. Basis is **identified** opportunities (documented in the findings report), never contracted results, which depend on client action.

**Option B — the fee-match reduction (recommended starting posture):**
> If identified savings opportunities are less than [3x] our fee, we reduce the fee to [half]. If we identify nothing worth acting on, you owe nothing.
Two-step structure caps exposure while keeping the headline ("if we find nothing, you pay nothing"). The zero-findings clause is the quotable part and, per the data, may essentially never trigger.

**Option C — the credit (safest, weakest marketing):**
> Any shortfall against [2x] fee is credited toward future Prescription Benefit Solutions work.
Lowest risk, converts a miss into a relationship. Weakest as an ad claim. Suitable if the data shows a real low-findings tail.

## 3. Guardrails that apply to any option (counsel reviews these)

- **Covered scope:** full Contract Review / PBR on plans ≥[500] lives with ≥[12] months of claims data provided; the Renewal Second Opinion is EXCLUDED from the guarantee at launch (narrower scope, less room to find).
- **"Identified" defined in the engagement letter:** savings opportunities documented in the written findings report with stated dollar magnitude and the contract or claims basis for each; the measure is what we FIND, not what the client implements.
- **Data condition:** the guarantee is void if the plan cannot supply the contract and claims data the scope requires (we cannot find what we cannot see).
- **One guarantee per client per 12 months;** guarantee language appears in the engagement letter, not just marketing.
- **Public-copy rule:** guarantee claims always appear with the basis sentence ("identified in the written findings report") and never with the $78.7M contracted figure in the same breath, keeping identified vs contracted clean per the standing proof-metrics guardrails.

## 4. Where it deploys once approved

Contract Review page + "How we charge" page (pricing memo Option copy has the slot) · Email 5's offer paragraph (one added sentence) · the sales playbook's price objection · paid ads ONLY after counsel approves the exact ad phrasing. Never in cold copy toward brokers (it is a buyer offer; the partner track sells collaboration, not discounts).

## 5. The honest counterargument (so the decision is real)

A guarantee anchors conversations on fee risk instead of value, and a sophisticated buyer may read it as insecurity. Mitigation: the guarantee lives one layer down (page copy and calls, not the hero headline); the hero remains the record. If Ginny is lukewarm after the data pull, skip it: the Standards + Fiduciary File already carry the differentiation, and the guarantee can launch any quarter the numbers support it.
