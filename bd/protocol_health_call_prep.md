## Anchor thesis (open with this)
Pharmacy savings have been picked over. The unmanaged frontier is the drugs on the **medical claim**, and the win isn't a better rebate, it's moving from **retrospective discovery to prospective control** at the point of prescribing and site selection.

## Four talking points (the "wisdom" he's sensing)
1. **Visibility first.** J-codes bill under the medical benefit, bypass the PBM's edits, and the plan never sees them until the EOB. You can't manage what you can't see, integrate medical and Rx claims.
2. **Site of care is the biggest single lever.** The same infusion can cost dramatically more in a hospital outpatient setting than in an office or home/independent suite. Steerage at initiation beats any rebate.
3. **Product selection at the first order.** Oncology and infused biologics now have biosimilars, but the medical side often defaults to the higher-cost originator. The money is in preferred-product/biosimilar selection before therapy starts.
4. **Prospective vs. retrospective (his exact pain).** Medical PA plus point-of-prescribing review catches it before the regimen and site lock in. Retrospective review only documents the loss.

## Diagnostic questions to ask Rob
