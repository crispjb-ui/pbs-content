# Potter Topic Bank — 20 future pieces worthy of the channel

**Created:** July 6, 2026. The reservoir feeding the quarterly-column cadence (`wendell_potter_post_series_plan.md` §3) after the 13-piece roster + "Follow the Ownership" run. Every entry passes the channel's bar: a genuinely under-known structural mechanism, named actors, real policy stakes, a prescription a regulator could actually execute, and a PBS cascade angle. **Draft any entry via `/draft-potter-piece`** (the outline-era rhetorical habits do not apply; the corrected standard governs). `/quarterly-research` promotes entries when their news peg fires; every draft gets `/verify-fact` at submission week and `/red-team` before publish. None of these duplicates the drafted roster (spread, vertical integration, GLP-1, accumulators, white-bagging, FTC follow-on, aggregators, DIR successors, specialty routing, assistance diversion, RFP, audit rights, ownership).

**Format per entry:** metaphor seed · thesis · named actors · prescription direction · cascade angle · peg/timing · verify-heavy?

## Cluster A — The contract machinery (closest to PBS's proprietary ground)

**1. The Average That Cannot Lose (effective-rate guarantees).** *Metaphor: a casino that guarantees you win "on average," measured by the casino's math, across games it chooses, after removing the tables you won at.* Thesis: GER/BER blended guarantees are engineered to be unfalsifiable — exclusions, offsetting, and self-reconciliation make the promise mathematically true and economically empty. Actors: big-3 contract templates (pattern, not incident). Rx: guarantee-verifiability floor (named benchmark, closed exclusions, claim-level reconciliation rights). Cascade: GER audit toolkit (exists). Peg: evergreen; strong post-Index (Dimension 2 data). Verify: light (PBS observation register).

**2. The Hotel California Clause (auto-renewal + exit economics).** *Metaphor: a gym membership that renews for three years unless you cancel during one week nobody tells you about, and charges you to get your workout history back.* Thesis: exit is priced and timed out of existence: auto-renew cliffs, rebate forfeitures, data-return fees, de-implementation charges. Actors: pattern-wide. Rx: federal floor on renewal/exit terms (notice symmetry, no rebate forfeiture, free standard-format data return). Cascade: Termination Clause toolkit (exists); 90-Day Check season. Peg: **August/September (renewal season)** — the natural first quarterly piece of fall. Verify: light.

**3. The Allowance That Owns You (implementation allowances as golden handcuffs).** *Metaphor: a signing bonus that becomes a debt the day you think about leaving.* Thesis: upfront "allowances" amortize invisibly across the term and claw back on exit, converting a sweetener into a switching cost that outlives the pricing's competitiveness. Actors: pattern-wide; consultant-market dynamics that spotlight year-one economics. Rx: disclosure of amortization schedules + clawback caps. Cascade: renewal-leverage content. Peg: renewal season or post-Index. Verify: light.

**4. Everyone Is Transparent Now (the co-opting of the word).** *Metaphor: when every casino in town renames itself "The Honest House," the word has stopped carrying information.* Thesis: "transparent" and "pass-through" became marketing categories without operational tests; the test is contractual (definitions, reconciliation rights, intermediary disclosure), and almost no "transparent" offering passes it. Actors: the market broadly, incl. transparent-model entrants measured against their own claims. Rx: an operational definition regulators could adopt (previews the Index + Standards without self-promotion). Peg: **January, WITH the Index** — the natural companion op-ed. Verify: medium (survey of public claims).

**5. Pass-Through's Fine Print (the per-script toll).** *Metaphor: a toll-free highway funded by a charge on every car.* Thesis: pass-through pricing moved the margin from spread to admin-fee-per-claim and program fees; honest at the drug level, reconstructed at the invoice level. Actors: pattern-wide. Rx: all-in per-claim cost disclosure standard. Cascade: admin-fee decoder assets (exist). Peg: evergreen. Verify: light.

## Cluster B — The money's newer hiding places

**6. The Biosimilar That Couldn't Win (the rebate trap).** *Metaphor: a store that stocks the $1,000 version on the eye-level shelf because the manufacturer pays for the shelf, while the $200 version sits in the back room.* Thesis: high-list, high-rebate originators held formulary position against far cheaper biosimilars for years (the adalimumab record is the documented case); rebate economics beat plan economics whenever the middleman keeps a share. Actors: big-3 formulary decisions, documented publicly in the Humira-biosimilar era. Rx: net-cost-based formulary duty for any intermediary taking rebate-linked compensation. Cascade: biosimilar readiness toolkit (exists). Peg: any biosimilar-launch news cycle. Verify: medium (public record strong).

**7. Bona Fide (the definitional war of 2028).** *Metaphor: a tax code that exempts "legitimate business expenses," defined by the taxpayer.* Thesis: with federal pass-through reform phasing in 2028-29, the entire game moves into one phrase, "bona fide service fees," and the reclassification of rebate-economics into exempt fee categories is being engineered NOW; watch the war before it happens. Actors: PCMA advocacy; big-3 fee schedules. Rx: effects-based definition (any manufacturer payment tied to utilization or position flows to the plan, whatever it is called — the series' refrain applied to the new law). Peg: **timely for 2-3 years; ideal when implementing regs draft.** Verify: HEAVY (statute + rulemaking status at submission).

**8. Your Claims, Their Asset (the data-ownership inversion).** *Metaphor: a bookkeeper who insists your ledger is his property and sells copies of it.* Thesis: plans generate the claims data, PBMs claim ownership, monetize de-identified data downstream, and charge plans extraction fees for their own numbers; data terms are the least-negotiated clauses with the longest consequences. Actors: contract patterns; data-licensing revenue streams. Rx: statutory plan ownership of claims data + free standard-format portability. Cascade: data-rights contract language (Library family). Peg: evergreen; pairs with any AI-in-healthcare news. Verify: light.

**9. The Million-Dollar Claim Meets the Middleman (gene therapy).** *Metaphor: financing a house through a mortgage broker who also owns the house, the appraiser, and the inspector.* Thesis: gene-therapy financing models (installments, outcomes-based refunds, carve-out "solutions") are being built with the same conflicted intermediaries in the middle, before plans understand the products; the GLP-1 lesson at 100x severity per claim. Actors: PBM-affiliated gene-therapy programs. Rx: outcomes-verification independence + carve-out transparency floor. Cascade: pipeline watch-list toolkit (exists); the Piece-3 seed paid off. Peg: any FDA gene-therapy approval wave. Verify: medium.

**10. The Cap That Moved the Cost (Part D spillover).** *Metaphor: squeezing a balloon in the government's fist and watching it bulge into the employer's.* Thesis: the Medicare out-of-pocket redesign shifted manufacturer and plan liabilities in ways that repriced commercial contracts too (rebate recalibration, catastrophic-phase economics), and employer plans absorbed repricing they never traced to its statutory source. Actors: structural; manufacturers + PBMs recalibrating. Rx: commercial-market disclosure of redesign-driven term changes. Peg: annual, at Part D bid/renewal news. Verify: HEAVY (policy detail moves).

## Cluster C — The people the machine touches (Potter's home register)

**11. The Pharmacy Desert Your Contract Helped Create.** *Metaphor: a town square where every shop is a franchise of the toll collector.* Thesis: below-cost reimbursement to independents, steering to owned channels, and post-hoc fees closed thousands of community pharmacies; network adequacy is now a patient-access and plan-disruption problem the contracts caused. Actors: big-3 reimbursement + steering practices; the FTC's own findings. Rx: reimbursement floors tied to acquisition cost + network-adequacy standards with teeth. Cascade: member-communication and network toolkits (exist). Peg: any closure-wave story; rural-health news. Verify: medium.

**12. The Prescription Your Doctor Didn't Choose (prescriber-side integration).** *Metaphor: a waiter who owns the kitchen, writes the menu, and works for the landlord.* Thesis: the same parents that own PBMs and pharmacies now employ tens of thousands of physicians; when the prescriber, the coverage rule, and the dispensing channel share one parent, "clinical judgment" becomes a supply-chain decision. Actors: UnitedHealth/Optum's physician employment at national scale; peers following. Rx: prescribing-independence firewalls + referral-pattern disclosure. Peg: any physician-acquisition news. Verify: medium (employment figures move; characterize scale generally).

**13. The Mail-Order Medicine You Didn't Order.** *Metaphor: a book club that ships you the book whether you finished the last one or not, and bills your employer either way.* Thesis: auto-refill economics, 90-day mandates, and returned-drug non-crediting make waste a revenue line; the plan pays for adherence theater. Actors: PBM-owned mail operations. Rx: refill-consent standards + returned/unused crediting rules. Cascade: utilization-anomaly content. Peg: evergreen; strong with any waste investigation. Verify: light.

**14. The Refund the States Got and You Didn't.** *Metaphor: your neighbor sued the contractor, proved the overcharge, and got paid; you hired the same contractor and never asked.* Thesis: state Medicaid programs recovered enormous spread-pricing settlements (the Centene wave is public record) using exactly the claims-level analysis commercial employers rarely run; the playbook is proven, public, and unused by the buyers with the most at stake. Actors: the settled conduct (public record); the inertia. Rx: commercial-market claims-audit floor; state AG playbook extension. Cascade: the strongest possible "run the audit" pairing. Peg: any new state settlement. Verify: medium (settlement record solid).

## Cluster D — The system's defenses (how reform actually gets stopped)

**15. The 1974 Law That Became a Shield (ERISA preemption).** *Metaphor: a fire code that applies to every building except the ones where most people live.* Thesis: the series' recurring wall gets its own piece: how a law written to protect pension portability became the reason every state PBM reform stops at the self-funded border, and what the post-*Rutledge* line of cases actually permits. Actors: structural; PCMA's preemption litigation strategy. Rx: the federal-backstop model (state floors extended to ERISA plans by federal adoption). Peg: any preemption ruling. Verify: HEAVY (case law precision required; counsel-adjacent).

**16. The Study Money Buys (the evidence machine).** *Metaphor: the tobacco institute, with better graphics.* Thesis: the PBM-funded research ecosystem (commissioned studies, sponsored white papers, friendly economics) manufactures the citations reform opponents wave; reading the funding line is a civic skill. Actors: PCMA-commissioned literature vs. FTC/academic findings, compared concretely. Rx: funding-disclosure norms for testimony citations. Peg: whenever a "PBMs save $X" study circulates. Verify: medium; broker-safe, but red-team the specific-study characterizations hard.

**17. The Comment Letter Nobody Reads (how rulemaking gets captured).** *Metaphor: a suggestion box where one commenter files 400 pages with exhibits and everyone else writes a postcard.* Thesis: the asymmetry of administrative participation, who staffs the dockets, decides the fine print that decides the money; written as a participatory call: plan sponsors and independent experts showing up changes outcomes (PBS's own comment-letter lane modeled without self-congratulation). Rx: procedural (docket participation, small-entity outreach). Peg: any open PBM docket. Verify: light.

## Cluster E — The advisor economy (HANDLE WITH THE GUARDRAIL)

**18. Who Pays Your Advisor (the compensation-disclosure gap).** *Metaphor: a home inspector whose fee depends on which house you buy.* Thesis: the ESI consent order's consultant-compensation disclosure requirement named a real structural issue: undisclosed PBM-to-advisor payments; **the frame is that disclosure VALIDATES the aligned advisors** (the ones who disclose everything win business from this reform) and the PBM is the party that built the hidden channel. Actors: the PBM-side payment structures; never named brokerages. Rx: universal compensation disclosure in ERISA advice. **RED-TEAM ATTACK 3 MANDATORY; the W14 tripwire lives here.** Cascade: pairs with the approved broker-comp angle (issue #42 item ④), same guardrail. Peg: consent-order implementation news. Verify: medium.

**19. The Coalition Paradox (group purchasing's fine print).** *Metaphor: a carpool where the driver owns the gas station.* Thesis: employer coalitions and purchasing groups promise leverage, and sometimes deliver it, but the contract behind the coalition is negotiated once, owned by the aggregator, and rarely auditable by the member employer; scale without sight is just concentration. Actors: structural (aggregation models broadly, incl. PBM-owned group arrangements). Rx: member-level audit and exit rights in coalition contracts. Cascade: strong "questions to ask your coalition" asset. Peg: evergreen. Verify: light-medium.
