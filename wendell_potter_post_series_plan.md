# After the Twelve — where the Potter series goes when it lands

**Created:** July 6, 2026 (Brett's question). The 12-piece roster runs roughly through late 2026 or beyond depending on Wendell's cadence (currently on hold pending his confirmation). This is the plan for the day Piece 12 publishes, so the series ends as a launchpad, not a stop.

## 1. The anthology: "Inside the PBM Black Box"

The series was architected as one continuous story (three acts, recurring spine, callbacks and handoffs). That structure means the twelve pieces plus the Opening ARE a book-shaped manuscript the day they finish. Compile them, add a short new introduction and a closing reform-agenda chapter that reassembles all twelve prescriptions into one legislative checklist, and publish as: a canonical anthology page on rxbs.org (the AEO asset of record for "PBM reform"), a designed PDF (toolkit system, gated as the top-tier lead magnet), and an offer to Wendell to co-brand the compilation for HEALTH CARE un-covered's audience. Effort is low because the writing exists; the compile is an editing pass plus two new short chapters.

## 2. The Index becomes the sequel

The series argues the case; the Transparency Index (publishing mid-January, right as the roster ends) measures it annually. The natural pitch to Wendell: a standing arrangement where each January's Index gets its first-look piece in his outlet, written by Ginny. That converts a 12-piece engagement into a permanent annual slot tied to PBS's flagship asset, with fresh data each year instead of a topic list that eventually exhausts.

## 3. Cadence conversion: from series to column

After the roster, propose shifting from "series with an end" to a quarterly rhythm: one piece per quarter tied to whatever the quarterly landscape research surfaces (the "Follow the ownership" piece already approved in the queue is the first of these), plus the January Index piece. Quarterly is sustainable indefinitely, keeps the byline warm, and matches when there is genuinely new material worth his audience's time. The queued-candidate mechanism in `wendell_potter_piece_outlines.md` already feeds this.

## 4. The syndication ladder

Twelve published policy pieces plus an anthology is a writing portfolio. Use it to climb: pitch adapted versions or fresh pieces to STAT First Opinion and Health Affairs Forefront (both take practitioner-authored PBM pieces and both are heavily cited by the policy community and by AI engines), then trade press guest columns (BenefitsPro, EBN) for the buyer audience. Each placement is a branded mention on a new authority domain, which is precisely what the entity-authority plan needs. The anthology is the credential attached to every pitch.

## 5. Testimony readiness

The series plus the Index constitute a testimony CV: state legislatures running PBM bills need exactly one witness who reviews contracts for a living and has published the mechanics plainly. When the comment-letter lane (category blueprint, Mechanic 3) produces its first docket entries, pair them with an offer to testify. One state hearing produces citable public record, video for the clip pipeline, and standing no competitor in the size band has.
