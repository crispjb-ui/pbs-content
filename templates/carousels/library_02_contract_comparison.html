Article IV · § 4.02 · Pricing and Payment
Definition of Rebate Pass-Through
Rebate Pass-Through.

The PBM will pass through a portion of manufacturer rebates attributable to Plan Sponsor, in accordance with PBM's standard rebate methodology 100 percent of all Manufacturer Payments, including rebates, administrative fees, market-share payments, data fees, and inflation protection, received by the PBM or any Affiliate in connection with Plan Sponsor's claims. Reconciliation statements will be delivered quarterly with claim-level detail.

Why it matters
If the PBM controls what counts as "eligible," the passthrough guarantee is whatever they want it to be. Anchor it to every revenue category, by name.

Article IV · § 4.01 · Pricing — Generic and Brand Discount
AWP Discount Base and Exclusions
Brand Discount.

Brand pricing guarantees are calculated as an AWP discount subject to standard industry exclusions as determined by PBM applied to ingredient cost at time of dispensing, as published by First Databank, before any dispensing or administrative fees. Excluded categories are limited to specialty drugs dispensed by a non-affiliated limited distribution pharmacy, clearly listed by NDC in Exhibit C, and may not be expanded without Plan Sponsor written consent.

Why it matters
"Standard industry exclusions" is a blank check. Excluded categories move the real guarantee by hundreds of basis points. Name every exclusion; publish the NDC list.

Article IV · § 4.05 · Guarantee Reconciliation
Remediation Timing and Method
Guarantee Reconciliation.

If a guarantee is not met, PBM shall remediate in accordance with its standard reconciliation process PBM shall deliver a reconciliation report within 60 days of each contract year-end with claim-level supporting detail. Any shortfall shall be paid in cash to Plan Sponsor within 30 days of report delivery. Plan Sponsor retains the right to an independent audit of the reconciliation at PBM's expense.

Why it matters
A "standard reconciliation process" you cannot see is not a remediation mechanism. Require the timeline, the supporting data, and the payment terms in writing.

Article IX · § 9.03 · Termination — Data Transfer
Exit Rights and Cost
Data Transfer Upon Termination.

Upon termination, PBM will provide reasonable transition assistance subject to customary fees PBM shall provide, within 45 days and at no cost to Plan Sponsor, complete claim history, member eligibility, accumulators, prior authorization files, and any data necessary for a successor PBM to administer the benefit. PBM shall not condition data transfer on any payment or waiver.

Why it matters
"Customary fees" for data transfer is how incumbents hold renewal leverage. Strip the fee, set the deadline, remove the condition.

Article X · § 10.01 · Audit Rights
Plan Sponsor Audit Rights
Audit.

Plan Sponsor may audit PBM's records once per contract term at Plan Sponsor's expense, subject to PBM's customary audit protocols or its designated auditor, twice per contract year, upon 30 days written notice. PBM shall not restrict the audit scope, the auditor's identity, or the format of the records produced. Audit findings are binding on PBM unless contested within 30 days.

Why it matters
"Once per term" and "customary protocols" means the audit happens on the PBM's terms. The strongest contracts we review write the frequency, scope, and format into the clause itself.
