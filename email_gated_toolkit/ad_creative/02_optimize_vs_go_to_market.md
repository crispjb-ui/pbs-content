## Audience targeting

Same base audience as #1 (Contract Review Readiness), but with one tightening:

**Job title additions:** Director of Strategy · VP of Strategy (these decision-makers care about strategic frameworks, not just operational audits)

**Custom question on Lead Gen Form:**
- "Where are you in the PBM evaluation cycle?" (dropdown)
  - Considering whether to issue an RFP
  - Currently in RFP process
  - Just completed an RFP
  - Not currently evaluating PBMs
  - Other

This question segments leads by where they are in the engagement cycle. Reply rate on Email 5 will be highest from leads who are "considering whether to issue an RFP" — they self-identify as actively making the optimize-vs-go-to-market decision.

## Ad creative

### Image (1200 × 627 px)

**Concept: Two-Path Whiteboard with Decision Tree Annotation**

> A photorealistic straight-on photo, 1200 x 627 pixels. A clean white office whiteboard fills the frame, slight angle (1-2 degrees off-axis), faint board-marker residue from prior writing partially erased. Soft natural overhead daylight. A black dry-erase marker rests in the bottom-left tray.
>
> The whiteboard contains a hand-drawn decision tree:
>
> Top-center: in larger black-marker handwriting, double-underlined: "Optimize Existing or Go to Market?"
>
> A central question diamond, hand-drawn: "Audit findings > 5% of pharmacy spend?"
>
> Two branches:
>   Left branch (toward "OPTIMIZE EXISTING" written in green marker at the bottom-left):
>     - "Annual gap < 3%" (green check)
>     - "PBM responsiveness > 12/20"
>     - "→ amendment letter"
>
>   Right branch (toward "GO TO MARKET" written in red marker at the bottom-right):
>     - "Annual gap > 7%" (red flag)
>     - "PBM negotiability < 12/20"
>     - "→ RFP process"
>
> Connecting all branches: a small note in red marker at the bottom-center: "Either way: schedule next PBR."
>
> Below the decision tree, in smaller handwriting: "PBS reviews 100s of PBM contracts a year. ~70% optimize. ~30% go to market."
>
> Style: realistic dry-erase whiteboard photography, soft natural daylight, faint surface texture and ghost-erase marks visible. Slightly imperfect handwriting (Caveat / Permanent Marker / Kalam-style). Colors: white whiteboard, black dry-erase marker for primary text, green dry-erase for the optimize path, red dry-erase for the go-to-market path. No logos, no client names.

## Why this is #2

Two ways to read this ad creative ranking:

(a) Sequentially after #1 — readers who downloaded the Contract Review Readiness Checklist may convert better on this ad in retargeting (they know what a Contract Review is; now they want the framework that comes out of it)

(b) Independently — readers who are already mid-cycle (e.g., "currently in RFP process") see this ad and self-identify as the right buyer for the post-Contract-Review framework

Either path produces a high-quality lead. The "where in the evaluation cycle" custom question segments these leads automatically for follow-up prioritization.
