---

## Why this is Tier 2 #2

Rebate is the #2 most-asked PBM topic in PBS's audience. Combined with 2026 regulatory pressure on PBM rebate practices (FTC investigations, state-level transparency requirements), the topic has cultural tailwind that Channel Pricing does not. The category-reclassification angle ("most plans lose pass-through dollars to administrative-fee reclassification") is the specific provocation that resonates with finance leaders.

This ad's lead profile skews CFO / Finance leader more than Channel Pricing's lead profile, which skews benefits operations.

---

Same base as #4 (Channel Pricing). Tighten:

## Ad creative

### Image (1200 × 627 px)

**Concept: Marked-Up Contract Page Photoreal — Rebate Definitions Section**

> A photorealistic top-down photo, 1200 x 627 pixels. An open printed PBM contract page rests on a dark wood desk under soft natural daylight. The page is slightly off-white (#F5F0E4) with a faint horizontal section header in bold black serif type at the top: "§ 4.07 DEFINITIONS OF MANUFACTURER PAYMENTS."
>
> Below the header, five vertically stacked term entries in Plex Mono Medium black:
>   1. Gross Rebate
>   2. Net Pass-Through Rebate
>   3. Price Protection Rebate
>   4. Administrative Fee
>   5. Drug Education / Data Licensing Payment
>
> Each term has a short typed definition snippet below it (rendered as recognizable legalese without specific words being legible).
>
> Five handwritten red ballpoint annotations:
>   - A yellow highlighter pass over the Gross Rebate definition with a red note: "The headline number"
>   - A red circle around the percentage in the Net Pass-Through definition with a red note: "Compare to actual. The gap is real."
>   - A red circle around "Excluded" in the Price Protection clause with a red note: "Carved OUT of pass-through"
>   - A red circle around "Outside" in the Administrative Fee clause with a red note: "Outside pass-through"
>   - A red circle around "Not classified" in the Drug Education clause with a red note: "Reclassified, retained."
>
> Top of page, in larger handwritten black marker, double-underlined: "Five rebate terms. Five definitions the PBM wrote."
>
> Below in handwritten red pen: "Four sit outside the pass-through clause."
>
> At the bottom, smaller red pen: "PBS reviews 100s of PBM contracts a year. The gap is the pattern."
>
> A black ballpoint pen rests at an angle in the top-right corner.
>
> Style: realistic paper texture, soft natural daylight, slightly imperfect handwriting. Colors: off-white contract paper, faint black legalese, red handwritten ballpoint, yellow highlighter, dark wood desk. No logos, no PBM names.
