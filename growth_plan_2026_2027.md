# PBS Growth Acceleration Plan — H2 2026 through 2027

**Created:** July 5, 2026, at Brett's request. This is the business-level plan that sits ABOVE the content system: how Prescription Benefit Solutions compounds its 2025 base (706 engagements, 203 clients, $78.7M contracted savings) into a durably larger, less concentrated, more valuable firm. It synthesizes and sequences everything already diagnosed and built (`strategy_audit_2026_07.md`, the conversion infrastructure, the target-account layer) and adds the 2027 business-model horizon. Internal doc; PBS shorthand fine.

**How to read it:** three horizons, each with a small number of moves, each move with an owner and a decision gate. Nothing here fights for the same hours twice; the whole plan is built around the binding constraint.

## 1. The starting position (facts, not aspiration)

- **Delivery excellence is proven at scale:** 706 engagements / 203 clients / $78.7M contracted in 2025; PBR line averages $469K savings per client; RFP line runs 25%.

- **The audience asset is real and growing:** 2,100+ LinkedIn followers gaining ~50-100/wk in season, 1,086 newsletter subs at 44-49% open, a repeatable organic content shape (15-18K reach), earned-media credentials (Potter, SHRM), and a live 29-toolkit lead funnel that has already produced a signed client (Stand Together), a forming broker partnership (GWCU), and inbound leads.
- **The conversion layer just got built (Jul 3):** post-sequence nurture, renewal reactivation, broker partner track, target account list (~41 employers + 22 broker firms), quarterly briefing playbook, closeout/proof kit, sales hour + KPI loop, 21 automation workflows.
- **The binding constraint is Ginny's hours,** not ideas, not content, not even leads. Every move below either respects that constraint or spends money/process to relax it.

## 5. Horizon 3 — COMPOUND (2027): the business-model moves

These change what PBS *is*, not just how it markets. Each is a proposal with a decision gate; none starts before its trigger.

**5.1 Formalize the product ladder.** Today the offer set reads as bespoke consulting. Make the ladder explicit and priceable:
- **Entry: Renewal Second Opinion** (fixed scope, fast, seasonal spikes) — the low-friction first yes.
- **Core: Contract Review** (the 4-6 week engagement Email 05 already describes: findings + fiduciary record + optimize-vs-RFP recommendation).
- **Recurring: PBM Oversight (PBR cadence)** — the twice-yearly Pharmacy Benefit Review packaged as an ongoing oversight retainer: quarterly reporting reviews, renewal-window watch, amendment redlines, a standing seat at the benefits-committee table. **This is the most valuable move in the plan:** recurring oversight smooths lumpy project revenue, deepens fiduciary-duty stickiness (the client's prudent-process record depends on it), and raises firm value.
- **Expansion: RFP management** (the 25%-savings line) when the review recommends going to market.
The content system then sells the ladder deliberately: every asset CTA names the rung it feeds. *(Gate: Ginny confirms names, scopes, and pricing posture; Claude drafts the one-pagers + site pages.)*

**5.2 Name the methodology.** "We review your contract" is a service; a **named, versioned methodology** (the framework behind the 132-review PBR line: the audit passes, the definitions library, the scoring) is an asset. Naming it raises perceived value, makes referrals repeatable ("get the [name] done before you sign"), feeds the annual report ("findings from N [name] reviews"), and is a prerequisite for 5.3. The Contract Language Library and 29 toolkits are already the public half of it; this consolidates the private half into a playbook.

**5.3 The capacity decision (the honest one).** If Horizons 1-2 work, demand will exceed Ginny. Two levers, in order:
- **Playbook-ize delivery** (5.2 makes this possible): standard work products, templates, the toolkit design system applied to deliverables — raising throughput per Ginny-hour before adding headcount.
- **Second clinical hire** (pharmacist analyst) — **trigger, not date:** sustained 6+ qualified conversations/month for a quarter, or delivery backlog pushing engagement starts past ~4 weeks. Revisit at each quarterly review; do not hire ahead of the trigger, do not refuse the trigger when it fires.
Brett's lane grows in parallel: sales ops (the Sheet, follow-ups, scheduling, briefing production) so Ginny's hours concentrate on delivery + relationships + camera.

**5.4 Widen the referral ecosystem beyond brokers.** Three adjacent lanes where PBS is the natural independent bench, all warm-intro-able, none requiring cold outreach:
- **ERISA / fiduciary counsel** (post-J&J-era fiduciary anxiety is the strongest tailwind in the category; attorneys need a defensible independent audit to point clients at; the fiduciary toolkit + documentation checklist are ready-made door-openers).
- **Stop-loss and captive advisors** (pharmacy drives their claims problem; a PBS review de-risks their book).
- **TPAs without pharmacy depth** (same gap as the broker bench).
One relationship per lane in H1 2027 as the experiment; the closeout kit's referral machinery already covers the ask. **Credit-union league circuit** (Carolinas league, LSCU) is the fourth lane: one presented session per league, feeding the CU account cluster.
*(Compliance posture on every lane: transparent independence, no referral-fee arrangements that would compromise the "no PBM money, no conflicts" brand; the currency is reciprocal expertise, not payments.)*

**5.5 Annual rhythm locks in.** By end-2027 the year has a shape competitors can't copy cheaply: **January** flagship data report + peak-window content push → **quarterly** live briefings + What We're Seeing reports → **May-June** mid-year claims/benchmark season → **September-October** renewal campaign (now in its second, bigger year) → **November-December** report data pull + planning. The content engine already runs this seasonally; this makes the *business* run it too.

## 7. What we will NOT do (focus discipline)

- No new social channels in 2026 (no TikTok/YouTube builds until video is weekly and easy).
- No paid Substack work (~12-month hold stands).
- No cold outreach, no contact scraping, no purchased lists — the brand IS the warm machine.
- No referral-fee schemes with brokers or counsel; independence is the moat.
- No hiring ahead of the 5.3 trigger; no front-loading Q4 content (the loop builds it).
- Never a move that positions PBS against brokers or competes with Hylant; diversification is alongside, not away.
