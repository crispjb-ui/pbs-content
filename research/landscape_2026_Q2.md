## The three biggest developments

1. **The FTC insulin case is over — all big-3 PBMs settled, OptumRx last (June 16).** The agency's signature PBM enforcement action resolved by consent, not trial.
2. **Federal PBM reform is now law** (Consolidated Appropriations Act 2026, signed Feb 3), phasing in 100% commercial rebate pass-through, bona-fide-service-fee-only compensation, any-willing-pharmacy access, and semiannual plan-sponsor reporting through 2028–2029.
3. **The rebate-driven PBM model is being dismantled in public** — the ESI consent order plus the "Net Pricing Drug Channel" shift mean net-cost / rebate-free contracts move from fringe to default in 2027–2028 bids. Plan sponsors who budget around rebate checks need a new plan.

## Developments (what happened · source · plan-sponsor implication)

### 1 · FTC insulin case fully settled — OptumRx is the last domino (Q2)
**What happened:** The FTC's 2023 case against the big-3 PBMs and their affiliated GPOs (Caremark/Zinc, Express Scripts/Ascent, OptumRx/Emisar) over insulin-rebate practices resolved entirely by settlement. Express Scripts settled Feb 4; CVS Caremark moved to withdraw from adjudication March 23; **OptumRx reached the final proposed settlement mid-June 2026, ending the FTC's campaign against the big three.** No trial, no liability finding — a "series of settlements" rather than a landmark ruling.
**Source:** [FTC–ESI settlement (FTC, Feb 2026)](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/02/ftc-secures-landmark-settlement-express-scripts-lower-drug-costs-american-patients) · [OptumRx final settlement (BenefitsPro, Jun 16 2026)](https://www.benefitspro.com/amp/2026/06/16/optum-rx-becomes-final-pbm-to-reach-settlement-with-ftc-over-insulin-pricing/) · [FTC campaign ends (HealthExec)](https://healthexec.com/topics/healthcare-management/legal-news/ftc-settles-lawsuit-optum-rx-ending-its-campaign-against-big-three-pbms) · [CVS settlement (Buchanan Ingersoll)](https://www.bipc.com/cvs-caremark-settles-ftc-insulin-pricing-lawsuit-key-developments-and-industry-impact)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** The enforcement threat that pressured the big-3 is largely spent; the durable pressure now comes from statute and contract, not litigation. For plan sponsors this means the leverage is *yours to exercise at renewal* — the settlements set behavior floors for the settling PBMs but adopting the new terms is optional and negotiated. Don't wait for a regulator to fix your contract.

### 2 · Federal PBM reform signed into law (context — Feb; implementation runs through Q2 and beyond)
**What happened:** Key provisions of the PBM Reform Act of 2025 (H.R. 4317) were folded into the Consolidated Appropriations Act of 2026 and signed Feb 3, 2026. For **commercial group health plans**: semiannual PBM reporting to plan sponsors detailing net drug spend, rebates, spread, and steering to PBM-affiliated pharmacies; **100% rebate pass-through** and related requirements phasing in **2028–2029**. Medicare Part D: remuneration restricted to flat-dollar **bona fide service fees** (2028); **any-willing-pharmacy** access on "reasonable and relevant" terms (2029).
**Source:** [Congress passes landmark PBM reform (Mintz, Feb 2026)](https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/2146/2026-02-06-congress-passes-landmark-pbm-reform-2026-spending-bill) · [Sidley Austin analysis](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2026/02/congress-passes-significant-federal-pharmacy-benefit-manager-reform-impacting-pharmaceutical-market) · [Buchanan Ingersoll: what it means for employers](https://www.bipc.com/sweeping-pbm-reforms-arrive-what-the-2026-federal-legislation-means-for-pharmacies,-patients-and-employers) · [Pharmacy Times](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/pbm-reform-within-2026-appropriations-bill-signed-into-law)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** The mandated semiannual report is a *contract-drafting opportunity now*, not a 2028 problem — plan sponsors should be amending reporting language this renewal so the statutory minimum is a floor they exceed, not a ceiling they wait for. The 100%-pass-through mandate also collides with rebate-dependent budgeting (see #5): the plans that win are the ones modeling the rebate-free future before it's forced.

### 3 · The state PBM-reform wave kept compounding in Q2 (12+ states in 2026)
**What happened:** At least a dozen states enacted significant new PBM laws in 2026 — compensation limits, transparency mandates, NADAC-based minimum reimbursement, and outright spread-pricing bans. **California** banned spread pricing effective Jan 1, 2026; **Virginia** (S.B. 669, signed March 31, effective **July 1, 2026**) mandates pass-through pricing, fee-only compensation on plan-sponsor request, and 100% rebate direction to the plan; **Alabama** banned spread and mandated 100% rebate pass-through; **Ohio** (H.B. 229) added audit authority over rebates and spread. All 50 states now regulate PBMs in some form.
**Source:** [States continue to advance PBM legislation (Mintz, Apr 2026)](https://www.mintz.com/insights-center/viewpoints/2146/2026-04-09-states-continue-advance-new-pbm-and-drug-pricing) · [Frier Levitt: 2026 state PBM reform, NADAC + spread bans](https://www.frierlevitt.com/articles/2026-state-pbm-reform-nadac-reimbursement-spread-pricing-bans/) · [NASHP state PBM tracker](https://nashp.org/state-tracker/state-pharmacy-benefit-manager-legislation/)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** For self-funded ERISA plans the state laws are mostly *indirect* (ERISA preemption limits direct reach), but they reset market norms — when spread is illegal for state-regulated plans in a dozen states, "we don't do spread" stops being a concession and becomes table stakes a self-funded plan can demand. The reveal framing writes itself: *when red and blue states pass the identical fix, it's not politics, it's the business model.*

### 4 · The ESI consent order rewrites the PBM economic model for plan sponsors
**What happened:** Beyond insulin, the ESI settlement forces structural change: a regulator-approved **standard option with net-price-based member cost-sharing**, limits on rebate-driven and spread pricing, automated plan-sponsor reporting (annual per-product cost disclosure, **claim-level pharmacy reporting**), and **full disclosure of any compensation paid to consultants or brokers.** The standard option is fully available Jan 1, 2027; point-of-sale rebates / elimination of rebate guarantees / spread-free pricing land by Jan 1, 2028. ESI's 2026–2027 bids are expected to increasingly feature rebate-free / net-cost models.
**Source:** [Gallagher: separating signal from noise](https://www.ajg.com/news-and-insights/the-ftc-express-scripts-settlement-in-pbm-reform/) · [Lockton: major shift in PBM pricing](https://global.lockton.com/us/en/news-insights/ftc-settlement-with-express-scripts-shift-in-pbm-pricing) · [Goodwin: fundamental changes to the PBM model](https://www.goodwinlaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/02/alerts-lifesciences-antc-express-scripts-settles-pbm-ftc-action)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** Two live actions. (a) **Rebate-check budgeting is now a liability** — plans that offset premiums with rebate checks face a net-cost world where that offset shrinks; model it before the 2027 bid. (b) The **broker/consultant compensation disclosure** term is a gift to plan sponsors and to the *good* brokers: it surfaces hidden PBM-to-consultant money that the aligned advisors never took. This *validates* the broker-as-partner frame (transparency helps the honest advisor) rather than undercutting it.

### 5 · Vertical-integration "shakeout" and the Net Pricing Drug Channel (Drug Channels, Q2)
**What happened:** Adam Fein's Drug Channels ran a Q2 series arguing the big-3 are **restructuring in response to client demand, legislation, and legal pressure**, with some beginning to *unwind* vertical integration, and an emerging **Net Pricing Drug Channel (NPDC)** where net prices (not list) drive access and economics as the gross-to-net bubble deflates. PBM-affiliated specialty pharmacies still capture ~two-thirds of pharmacy-dispensed specialty revenue; the big-3 still process ~80% of equivalent claims.
**Source:** [PBM Shakeout: how vertical integration is reshaping what's next (Drug Channels, May 2026)](https://www.drugchannels.net/2026/05/pbm-shakeout-how-vertical-integration.html) · [Net Pricing Revolution / gross-to-net bubble (Apr 2026)](https://www.drugchannels.net/2026/04/the-net-pricing-revolution-in-drug.html) · [DCI 2026 vertical-integration map (Jun 2026)](https://www.drugchannels.net/2026/06/mapping-vertical-integration-of.html)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** "Transparent" and "pass-through" are about to be everyone's pitch, including the incumbents' — which raises, not lowers, the value of an independent read of the actual contract. When net-cost becomes the marketing default, the differentiator is whether the *definitions* behind the net-cost promise hold up. That is exactly PBS's lane.

### 6 · MLR gaming via intercompany eliminations — a genuinely under-known structural mechanism
**What happened:** The mid-April Drug Channels roundup surfaced (via a Milliman paper commissioned by PhRMA) how vertically integrated insurers/PBMs reshape **Medical Loss Ratio** outcomes through **intercompany eliminations** — overpaying affiliated providers and retaining rebates inside the parent so profit shifts *out of* the MLR-regulated insurance entity (subject to 80–85% MLR) and *into* unregulated affiliated businesses. Same roundup: **nearly 40% of commercially insured members were in plans with copay maximizers in 2025.**
**Source:** [Drug Channels News Roundup, mid-April 2026 (MLR games + copay maximizers)](https://www.drugchannels.net/2026/04/drug-channels-news-roundup-mid-april.html)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** The MLR-elimination mechanism is a fresh "hidden structure" reveal the plan-sponsor audience genuinely does not know — the money doesn't disappear, it moves to a sibling company the regulator can't see. Auto-added to the shocking-fact bank (below). The 40% copay-maximizer prevalence refreshes an existing entry with a current figure.

### 7 · GLP-1 price resets + PBM formulary whiplash
**What happened:** Novo Nordisk announced (Feb 24) it will cut Wegovy and Ozempic list prices **up to 50%, to ~$675/month, effective Jan 1, 2027**; Eli Lilly is offering employers a net **~$449/month** multi-dose Zepbound. On formularies, **CVS Caremark flip-flopped** — set to exclude Zepbound July 1, then restoring Zepbound Oct 1 and adding Lilly's new obesity pill, with Lilly's and Novo's GLP-1s as **co-preferred** — while many employers narrowed coverage to diabetes indications and dropped standalone weight-management coverage.
**Source:** [Mercer: GLP-1 considerations for 2026](https://www.mercer.com/en-us/insights/us-health-news/glp-1-considerations-for-2026-your-questions-answered/) · [Are PBMs putting obesity drugs on formulary? (Managed Healthcare Exec)](https://www.managedhealthcareexecutive.com/view/are-pbms-putting-obesity-drugs-on-formulary-) · [CVS Caremark now covers both Zepbound and Wegovy (Clinical Nutrition Center)](https://www.clinicalnutritioncenter.com/research/cvs-caremark-covers-zepbound-wegovy-2026)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** GLP-1 remains the single biggest pharmacy-trend line and PBS's top topic-level amplifier (64K all-time ceiling). The story for plan sponsors: the *formulary* preference tracks rebate economics, not clinical value, and it can flip mid-cycle — the co-preferred reshuffle is a live example of "your formulary can change on you." Pair GLP-1 reach with a contract angle (mid-year formulary change notice; net-price vs. list on the new deals).

### 8 · Private-label biosimilars became the big-3's default — and a new transparency blind spot
**What happened:** The 2026 formularies lean hard on PBM-owned private-label subsidiaries — **Cordavis (CVS), Nuvaila (Optum), Quallent (ESI)** — for Humira and other biosimilars, priced to the parent's advantage rather than lowest-list. A formulary policy change alone moved share fast (Cordavis reached ~15% adalimumab share within 2 months of CVS's 2024 Humira exclusion). Employers can see **~80% per-prescription savings on Humira biosimilars even without rebates** — if they get the low-list product rather than the private-label mid-list one.
**Source:** [Big Three PBMs' 2026 formulary exclusions: private-label biosimilars (Drug Channels)](https://www.drugchannels.net/2026/01/the-big-three-pbms-2026-formulary.html) · [Private-label market-access channel: help or hinder? (JMCP)](https://www.jmcp.org/doi/10.18553/jmcp.2025.31.8.824)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** The private-label move is the vertical-integration story in miniature — the PBM now owns the "manufacturer" of the biosimilar it prefers, so the "preferred" pick can carry a *higher* list than an available low-list competitor. The plan-sponsor action: on every biosimilar, ask which specific product (branded / unbranded / private-label, and at what list) sits in the preferred slot, and why.

### 9 · The transparency ecosystem's Q2 focus: net pricing, MLR arbitrage, and "who owns the pharmacy"
**What happened:** Across Drug Channels (net-pricing revolution, VI shakeout, MLR games), Potter's HEALTH CARE un-covered (insurers now earning more from their PBMs than their health plans; CFOs assembling independent TPA + PBM + stop-loss + repricer stacks), and the FTC record, the shared throughline of the quarter is **follow the ownership** — rebates, specialty routing, private-label, and MLR all resolve to "the PBM owns the entity on the other side of the transaction."
**Source:** [Drug Channels (home)](https://www.drugchannels.net/) · [Potter: HEALTH CARE un-covered](https://healthcareuncovered.substack.com/) · [FTC PBM landing page](https://www.ftc.gov/terms/pharmacy-benefits-managers-pbm)
**Plan-sponsor implication:** PBS's whole thesis — the extraction is legal, contractual, and hidden in ownership — is exactly where the credible ecosystem now points. The reach shape that works (genuinely-unknown hidden-structure reveal + PBS anchor) is aligned with what the top analysts are publishing; keep welding a fresh structural fact onto each Thursday breakout.
