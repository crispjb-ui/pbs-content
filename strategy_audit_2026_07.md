**Date:** July 3, 2026
**Scope:** Full repo review (performance data, funnel infrastructure, content pipeline, strategy-vs-execution) + Ginny's July 3 answers on goals, follow-up process, capacity, and client sourcing.
**Period covered:** ~January–June 2026 (consistent posting began early January; funnel went live June).

---

## The One-Paragraph Verdict

PBS built a genuinely excellent content **engine** in six months: LinkedIn followers +190% (732 → 2,126), newsletter 444 → 1,086 at 44–49% open rates, a proven repeatable content shape (shocking hidden-structure reveal, ~15–18K organic even in the summer trough), a live 29-toolkit lead funnel with role scoring and hot-lead alerts, and real business outcomes already on the board (Stand Together signed, GWCU broker collaboration forming, first funnel-sourced meetings). The problem is no longer content. **The engine now outruns the conversion layer and the one human who operates it.** Every item that would turn attention into revenue — the AEO website pages, the paid-ads test, the post-Day-14 nurture, the measurement stack, the Sep–Oct surge material — is built, specced, and stalled in a manual queue. The next six months should be about **distribution and conversion, not more content**, and about attacking the single biggest business risk Ginny named: 95% of clients come through one broker. The most important data point of the entire six months is that the funnel has already produced a **second broker relationship** (GWCU). That is the play to industrialize.

## Part 1 — Strengths (keep doing, protect)

**1. The learning loop is the crown jewel.** Almost no small firm measures content this rigorously: conversion-per-10K-impressions by shape, save-maturation curves (3–4.3x after day 7), reader-actionability tests, Cuban likes-vs-reposts distinction. The shocking hidden-structure reveal (offshore GPO post: 18,523 impressions, fully organic, in the June trough) is a repeatable ~15–18K organic floor that no competitor in this niche has. Protect this loop; it compounds.

**2. The content machine is durable and cheap to run.** 10 weeks of pipeline, automated roundups, critique auto-fix, monthly research engines. Claude-side production is effectively solved. This means Ginny's time can and should shift almost entirely to the human-only work: video on camera, sales follow-up, Wix builds, relationships.

**3. The funnel produced real business in month one.** Stand Together (Contract Review + PBR, RFP this fall), GWCU broker (bulk-downloaded ~17 toolkits → June 22 meeting → collaboration forming), Sandeep (inbound, asked to pay), Chief People Officer intro via a podcast host. The mechanism works when it gets inflow.

**4. Earned-media credentials are real.** Wendell Potter contributorship (Piece 00 published June 17), SHRM Honest HR, Derms on Drugs. High-trust proof most consultancies never get.

**5. The 2025 proof metrics are a loaded weapon.** $78.7M contracted savings, 706 engagements, 203 clients — verified, guardrailed, and correctly held for the Sep–Oct surge. Nobody else in the space can claim solo numbers like this.

## Part 2 — Weaknesses (ranked by revenue impact)

**1. The conversion layer stops at Day 14.** After Email 5, a lead sits in a Google Sheet forever. No re-engagement drip, no repeat-download escalation, no renewal-season reactivation. Given that PBM decisions are seasonal (renewals cluster in fall), a lead captured in June who isn't ready until September is currently lost. This is the cheapest, highest-ROI fix available.

**2. Distribution is a single pipe: organic LinkedIn.** LinkedIn is login-walled (no search/AI crawl value), algorithm-dependent, and in a documented summer trough (weekly follower gains collapsed from +88–106 to +8–27). The three planned second pipes — paid ads (bank ready, $0 spent), AEO website pages (8 pages blueprint-complete, 0 built in Wix), and video (scripts ready, ~1 shipped) — are all stalled at the same gate: Ginny's manual queue.

**3. Measurement of the things that matter is dark.** The Q2 pipeline scorecard (DMs → meetings → closed) was never filled in. Search Console has never produced a data point (`GSC_SA_KEY` unset, sitemap unsubmitted). The AI-visibility baseline was never run. The strategy's own thesis ("be the cited source") is currently unfalsifiable. Total unblock cost: roughly one hour of Ginny's time.

**4. The Hylant concentration play isn't explicit in the strategy.** Ginny confirmed 95% of clients flow from one broker, and that this is *the reason* the content program exists. Yet "acquire 3–5 additional broker relationships" appears nowhere as a named objective with a number. The GWCU conversion proved the funnel can do it. It needs to be the headline goal, not a side effect.

**5. Paid Substack is a distraction.** 2 paid subs, ~$160, after 5 months, against a plan projecting $10–50K ARR. Meanwhile one consulting engagement averages $469K in client savings and five figures in fees. Substack's real jobs are (a) crawlable AI-citation surface and (b) free-sub lead capture. Recommend formally deprioritizing paid-tier work for 12 months.

**6. Ginny's manual load is misallocated.** A single week demands ~12 first-comments/reshares, 5–7 X posts/day, image generation, Wix builds, video. X in particular: ~35 posts/week of paste labor for 147 followers on a channel explicitly measured only by amplification events. That time is worth more in sales follow-up and video.

**7. The Sep–Oct surge is half-loaded.** Content is built through W37 (Sep 18); the surge window the strategy names runs through October. No Q4 calendar, and the flagship "2025 by the numbers" proof drop isn't built. If it gets built reactively in September, it ships weaker.

**8. Newsletter ≠ independent channel (accept and move on).** The data proved newsletter growth is mechanically ~0.6x of follower growth via LinkedIn's auto-prompt. Stop treating it as a separately-optimizable engine; optimize follower conversion instead.

5. **Earned media flywheel, warm only:** Potter cascade (12 pieces), natural podcast invites, and one ask per appearance ("who else should I talk to?"). Each appearance so far yielded a lead or credential; the flywheel works without cold pitching.

7. **Prune content breadth if load stays high:** the data says the working portfolio is (a) shocking-reveal Thursdays, (b) Monday deep dive + toolkit, (c) Library carousels (save-driven), (d) weekly video, (e) Wednesday POV. ## Part 8 — Risks to watch

- **Cuban dependency is manageable but real:** ~half the all-time top posts needed his repost; the organic ceiling is ~15–18K. Never plan a launch that *requires* amplification.
- **Broker-sensitivity tripwire:** the Broker Partner push increases exposure to the W14-type misstep (implying brokers missed something). Every broker-facing asset frames brokers as the heroes who bring PBS in.
- **Single-human failure mode:** the whole conversion layer currently routes through Ginny personally. The admin-scheduling pattern is the start of delegation; the broker kit and nurture emails continue it.
- **$78.7M claim compliance:** keep the guardrails (contracted-not-offered, 25%-is-RFP-rate, solo attribution) on every public use, especially paid ads — one sloppy ad screenshot lives forever.
