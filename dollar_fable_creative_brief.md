# "How a Dollar Travels Through a PBM" — creative brief (designer-ready)

**What it is:** the definitive illustrated telling of one plan-sponsor dollar's journey through the PBM system — a folded poster/booklet, beautifully produced, **physically mailed to the ~50 confirmed target accounts in early September** with a two-line note from Ginny, landing the same week the renewal campaign opens. Digital versions follow (LinkedIn carousel, Substack piece, site page) AFTER the mail drop so recipients had it first.

## The narrative (nine stations, each one spread/panel)

The dollar leaves the CFO's budget and passes through: (1) the plan's account, labeled and hopeful → (2) the claim, where **the definitions** decide what kind of dollar it is → (3) **the spread**, where the plan's price and the pharmacy's payment quietly diverge → (4) **the rebate detour**: the manufacturer's payment routes through a GPO/aggregator (offshore stamp on its passport) before a defined "share" comes back → (5) **the channel fork**: the same drug, three prices, routed to the owned pharmacy → (6) **the fee layer**: admin line items with five names and one purpose → (7) what arrives back at the plan, visibly lighter, with a cheerful summary report attached → (8) **the review**: a pharmacist with a flashlight walking the route backward, each station's paperwork examined → (9) the dollar's journey, mapped and accounted for: "you cannot recover what you cannot see; seeing is a choice."

Tone: wry, precise, never cartoonish about the money. The villain is the SYSTEM's architecture; no company names anywhere (this piece travels; keep it timeless and lawsuit-proof). Every station's caption is 1-2 sentences, plain English, with one real mechanism named per the shocking-fact bank (facts only from verified bank entries; no invented statistics anywhere).

## Visual + production spec

PBS v2 system: Primary Blue #015880 / Accent Blue #A7E0FA / Gray #4D4D4D on white; Plex Sans SemiBold display, Plex Mono for every dollar figure; the money-flow-map visual language the audience already responds to (the 17.8K-impression aggregator map is the stylistic ancestor). Format recommendation: **A3 poster, accordion-folded to A5**, station map on one face, "the review walks it backward" on the reverse; premium uncoated stock. rxbs.org and the wordmark small on the back panel with one line: *"We walk this route for a living. Prescription Benefit Solutions."*

**Mail package:** poster + Ginny's handwritten-style notecard (two lines, personalized to the account's situation from the target-list row: e.g., "Thought your committee might enjoy seeing the whole route before renewal season. If you ever want it walked backward, that is what we do.") No brochure, no pitch sheet — the restraint IS the message.

## Budget + logistics

Designer/illustrator: [$1-2K, one senior freelancer, 2-3 week turnaround; source from Ginny/Brett's network or a vetted marketplace]. Print + premium envelopes + postage for ~50: [$300-600]. Brett owns designer selection + production; admin owns the mail merge against the confirmed target list; Ginny writes/signs the 50 notecards (one sitting, ~90 min — this is deliberately not delegated; the handwriting is the point).

**Timeline:** brief to designer by early August ⏱ → proof by Aug 22 → print by Aug 29 → mail Sep 2-4 (lands with campaign week 1). Digital derivatives (carousel from the panels, Substack piece) slot into late September's calendar via the normal pipeline.

**Measurement:** each notecard references the briefing ("we're walking this live on the 16th") — briefing registrations from target-account domains are the mailer's conversion read. Log touches in the target-list rows.
