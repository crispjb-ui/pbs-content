## PHASE 3 — Ongoing (trust + maintenance)

### Step 3.1 — Recommendations: request 2-3 (the empty section that buys the most trust per minute)
Targets, per the broker-as-partner rule: one broker/consultant ally, one client-side CFO or HR leader, (optional) one former clinical colleague. Ask template (DM or email):

```
Hi [Name], I'm filling out the recommendations section on my LinkedIn
profile and a sentence or two from you would mean a lot. If it's easy,
something specific about [the contract language we tightened / the claims
review / the renewal prep] lands better than general praise. Happy to
write one for you in return.
```

What a good one names: a specific finding ("language in a contract I'd reviewed twice"), a working style, an outcome (anonymized dollar figures fine). Never imply a broker missed something; frame as partnership.

### Step 3.2 — Maintenance cadence
- **Quarterly:** refresh the subscriber counts in About/Experience (currently "1,000+"; bump wording at 1,500/2,000), confirm Featured links still render, swap the breakout-post slot if a newer post beats it.
- **When a flagship asset ships** (data report, major toolkit, podcast appearance): it competes for Featured slot 4; SHRM yields to bigger third-party credits.
- **Never:** banned hashtags/buzzwords in profile copy (same blacklist as posts); fabricated numbers; "Dr. Ginny Crisp."
