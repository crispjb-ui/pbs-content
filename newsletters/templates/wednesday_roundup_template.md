## Story Selection Criteria

When selecting 4-5 stories from the combined RSS + search results:

**Include (high priority):**
- PBM regulatory actions, lawsuits, or settlements (always relevant)
- FDA drug approvals that will affect employer pharmacy spend
- Biosimilar launches or interchangeability designations
- Drug pricing legislation or CMS rules affecting employer plans
- Court rulings on ERISA, fiduciary duty, or PBM practices
- Major PBM contract changes, mergers, or market shifts

**Include (medium priority):**
- GLP-1 developments (coverage, pricing, new indications)
- 340B program changes affecting employer claims
- Stop-loss market changes related to pharmacy
- State-level pharmacy benefit legislation

**Exclude:**
- Press releases without substance (new hire announcements, vague partnerships)
- Opinion pieces without data
- Stories older than 7 days
- Clinical trial results with no near-term formulary impact
- Manufacturer earnings reports (unless they reveal pricing strategy)
- Stories already covered in depth in Monday's deep dive that same week

## Humanize Review Process

Every article (Monday deep dive, Wednesday roundup, Thursday field note) must pass through the humanize review before publishing. This applies to both automated drafts and manually written content.

**How to run it:**
1. Open the draft file in Claude Code
2. Run: `/humanize newsletters/roundups/roundup_YYYY_MM_DD.md`
3. Review the changes Claude makes
4. Accept or adjust

**What the humanize review checks:**
- Removes AI-sounding language ("It's important to note," "Let's dive in," "navigate," "robust," "comprehensive," "actionable insights")
- Adds personal "I" and "we" voice where it reads too formal
- Varies sentence length (breaks up parallel structures)
- Removes em dashes used as sentence separators
- Ensures broker/carrier positive framing (PBM as adversary, brokers as partners)
- Checks that Ginny Crisp, PharmD attribution is correct (not "Dr. Ginny Crisp")
- Verifies company name is PBS (not RXBS)
- Adds conversational transitions between sections

**This step is non-negotiable.** AI-generated content (including automated roundup drafts) will read as AI-generated without this pass. The roundup in particular needs the humanize review because it is the most likely article to sound like a news aggregator instead of a clinical pharmacist with opinions.
