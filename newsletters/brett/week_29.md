## §4 · Saturday original — The $17M assistance loop (B1, Both Sides of the Counter)

```
At UNC I oversaw a medication assistance program that recovered more than $17 million a year. Manufacturer assistance, foundation grants, charity programs, matched to patients who could not afford their medications.

Health systems run these programs at industrial scale. There are whole teams whose job is knowing which manufacturer will cover which drug for which patient.

Now the part I did not appreciate until I moved to the benefits side. The employers paying for coverage rarely see any of this machinery. Assistance dollars move through the system in ways that never show up on a plan's claims report, even when the same dollars are shaping what the plan pays.

Two sophisticated parties, the health system and the employer, each seeing half the money.

If you work on either side of that split, I would genuinely like to hear how it looks from your seat.
```
