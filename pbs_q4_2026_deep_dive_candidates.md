### Candidate A — "Which PBM Reforms Actually Reach Your Plan?" (reform durability sorter)
- **Seed:** June 16, 2026 X exchange with @JdBell20 on the site-of-care/specialty post. JD listed the coming reform block (CAA 2026, list-price ban, delinking admin costs, DTC integration, ERISA). Ginny's question: how much sticks? The answer is a strong deep dive.
- **Thesis:** Most reform is *Medicare/Medicaid-bounded* and doesn't automatically reach commercial self-funded plans. The durable lever that DOES reach plan sponsors is **ERISA fiduciary pressure** (court-driven, not Congress-driven, puts liability on the sponsor directly). Whatever sticks federally, the structural mechanism lives in the *contract*, not the statute. Don't wait for the law to reach you; the contract is the lever you control now.
- **Sorting framework (the integrated tool):** a "Does this reach my commercial plan?" grid across the reform threads:
  - **Delinking admin from list price** → mostly Medicare Part D; commercial keeps retrospective AWP guarantees unless contractually restructured.
  - **CAA 2026 transparency + audit rights** → reaches commercial group health plans; but disclosure ≠ behavior change.
  - **ERISA fiduciary** → most durable, court-driven, hits the plan sponsor directly (J&J / Wells Fargo-style suits). The lever that doesn't need a vote.
  - **List-price ban / MFN** → contested, high litigation/implementation risk ("tied up in courts for decades").
  - **DTC integration (TrumpRx)** → parallel channel, not a reform of the existing PBM contract.
  - Tool output: for each reform, "reaches you? / durable? / what to do in your contract now."
- **Pillar:** Self-Funded Employer Guidance (or Transparency & Industry Education).
- **Pairs with W41:** both carry the same spine — "don't wait for the law, fix the contract." Could run as a late-Q4 companion (~W44-46) so the rebate-structure week and the reform-durability week bookend Q4 renewal season.
- **Differentiation from W19 (Fiduciary Responsibility):** W19 = fiduciary *documentation* checklist. This candidate = *reform durability sorting* (which statutes reach commercial plans). ERISA appears in both but the frame is distinct (documentation vs. what-reaches-you). Keep them from collapsing if both are in play.
- **News durability caveat:** reform specifics (CAA provisions, MFN status, DTC rollout) will move between now and Q4. Re-anchor facts at build time; keep the analytical frame (Medicare-bounded vs. ERISA-durable vs. contract-controlled), which holds regardless of which specific provisions survive.
- **Field Note pairing idea:** Thursday Field Note = "The ERISA Question Your Renewal Doesn't Mention" (the court-driven lever, plan-sponsor liability angle) supporting a Thursday LinkedIn decoder on the same.
