**Subject:** Proposed first-piece date + topic plan

Wendell,

Thanks again for the invitation. Below is what I can commit to in terms of cadence and the topic plan you asked for. Obviously deferring to your editor on sequence, timing, or anything else that fits your workflow better.

**Proposed cadence:** Every other Wednesday, starting Wednesday, May 27, 2026.

**Turnaround commitment:** I will deliver each piece draft to your editor with 7-10 days lead time before publish date for review and editorial passes. The lead time is a floor, not a ceiling. For the first piece specifically, I'll have the Spread Pricing draft to you by Wednesday, May 20.

Below is the topic plan I've drafted for the early run. I'm happy to share updated topic schedules a few weeks in advance as we work through these, so your editor always has runway on what's coming.

**Piece 1 · Wed May 27 · Spread Pricing: Why the FTC's Next Move Should Be Mandatory Disclosure.** Same drug, same dose, two prices, and the difference vanishes into PBM accounts the plan never sees. The FTC already has the data; this piece names the regulatory move that should follow.

**Piece 2 · Wed Jun 10 · Vertical Integration in PBMs.** Your PBM owns your specialty pharmacy, your insurer owns your PBM, and your PBM writes the rule that decides which pharmacy fills your member's high-cost drug. Three jobs, one vendor, and the bill that should break it up.

**Piece 3 · Wed Jun 24 · GLP-1 Coverage and the PBM Profit Layer.** The GLP-1 access debate everyone is having is the wrong debate. The list-price-to-net-cost gap on the highest-leverage drug class in modern memory is where the money is actually moving.

**Piece 4 · Wed Jul 8 · The Conflict of Interest at the Heart of Copay Accumulator Programs.** Manufacturer copay cards are a legitimate patient affordability tool and plan-sponsor accumulator programs can be a legitimate cost-containment lever, but the same PBM should not be controlling formulary placement, specialty pharmacy routing, and accumulator crediting math simultaneously. The reform is the structural firewall and member disclosure, not the prohibition.

**Piece 5 · Wed Jul 22 · White-Bagging and Channel Steering.** Same drug. Same dose. Same patient. Three different prices, all set by the same PBM, who is paid more when the pharmacy it chooses is its own.

**Piece 6 · Wed Aug 5 · The FTC PBM Report: What Should Happen Next.** In July 2024, the FTC published an interim staff report titled "Pharmacy Benefit Managers: The Powerful Middlemen Inflating Drug Costs and Squeezing Main Street Pharmacies." Nearly two years later there has been no final report and no comprehensive enforcement action; here are five named actions that should follow.

**Piece 7 · Wed Aug 19 · Rebate Aggregators: The Offshore Middlemen.** Ascent, Emisar, Zinc, and Premier are four PBM-owned entities most plan sponsors have never heard of, and they intercept manufacturer rebate flow before the pass-through math is calculated. A structural deception in plain sight.

**Piece 8 · Wed Sep 2 · DIR Fees and Their Successors.** The 2024 CMS rule banned retroactive DIR clawbacks in Medicare Part D, a real win. The PBMs are now reconstructing the same economic effect under new names; this piece names what to watch for.

**Piece 9 · Wed Sep 16 · Specialty Drug Routing Economics.** Beyond white-bagging, specialty routing is where vertical integration generates the largest hidden margin in the modern PBM contract. The PBM-owned specialty pharmacy does not compete on price; it competes on routing.

**Piece 10 · Wed Sep 30 · Manufacturer Patient Assistance Diversion.** A manufacturer foundation pays $5,000 to help a patient afford a specialty drug, and the PBM-owned specialty pharmacy is the gatekeeper, the formulary writer, and the entity that decides whether the assistance counts toward the deductible. Three conflicts, same vendor.

**Piece 11 · Wed Oct 14 · The PBM RFP Methodology Problem.** The PBMs know which scoring methodology your broker is using, and the headline pricing in their proposal is engineered to win that exact methodology. The information asymmetry is structural, and the fix is fiduciary clarity.

**Piece 12 · Wed Oct 28 · The Audit Rights Gap.** Plan sponsor audit rights in PBM contracts are technically present and operationally hollow: the windows are tight, the designees are restricted, the data extracts are PBM-defined. A federal audit-rights floor is the cleanest structural reform on the table.
