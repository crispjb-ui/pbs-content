## Performance Insights (Informing Q2 Strategy)

### Q1 Top Performers
- **Post 13 (GLP-1 Reality Check):** 41,594 impressions, 29,991 members reached (TEXT ONLY)
- **Tease Hook Format:** Consistently outperformed carousels and infographics
- **Friday Text Posts:** Higher engagement than polls

### Week 10 (Mar 9-13) Breakout Lessons
- **Messy Infographic ("PBM contracts have their own language"):** 22,047 impressions in 2 days (11,024/day), now all-time #3 post. Informal visual style signals authenticity and stops scroll better than polished carousels.
- **"Same drug. Same dose. Same member." carousel:** 12 saves in 1 day - short, repetitive hook structure drives bookmarking.
- **Tuesday carousel cannibalized by Monday newsletter:** "Audit clause nobody reads" got only 623 impressions in 3 days despite strong hook; same topic as Monday's "Audit Rights Gap" newsletter. Algorithm likely suppressed it as duplicate content for followers.
- **Newsletter Edition 4:** Best edition yet: 46% open rate, 362 email sends, 285 article views. Audit rights topic resonated.

### Q2 Format Strategy (Updated March 13)

**Structural Changes Based on Data:**

1. **Tuesday visual MUST differ from Monday newsletter topic.** The data shows same-topic overlap between Monday newsletter and Tuesday carousel causes cannibalization. Tuesday visuals now pull from a different pillar or take a distinctly different angle.

2. **Thursday visual carries the weekly theme.** Move the week's primary educational visual (carousel or infographic) to Thursday, 3 days after newsletter, no overlap risk.

3. **Messy infographic gets a regular slot.** ~Every other week, one visual slot (Tuesday or Thursday) should be messy infographic format. The informal/whiteboard style hit 22K impressions, the best performing visual format in our data.

4. **Tease hooks use short, repetitive structures.** "Same drug. Same dose. Same member." pattern works. Wednesday and Friday hooks should lead with this cadence when possible.

5. **Wednesday/Friday text stays pure text.** No images on text posts, confirmed by Q1 data.

**Updated Format Mix:**
- **Tuesday:** Visual from DIFFERENT pillar/angle than newsletter (carousel, infographic, or messy infographic)
- **Wednesday:** Tease hook or strong POV (no images) - short, repetitive hook structures
- **Thursday:** Weekly theme visual (carousel, infographic, or messy infographic)
- **Friday:** Tease hook teasing Monday newsletter (replaced polls)

## Engagement Optimization (Updated April 3 Based on 22-Post Dataset)

### High-Performance Tactics
1. **Tease hooks with specific numbers** - "$245", "1 in 12", "$2.1 million"
2. **Short, repetitive hook structures** - "Same drug. Same dose. Same member." cadence stops scroll
3. **Cut off before payoff** - Force "show more" click
4. **Monday newsletter references** - "More Monday" closing; also reference specific newsletter insights in Wednesday text
5. **First comment strategy** - Link to newsletter in first comment on carousels; link to Substack field note in Thursday first comment
6. **No images on text posts** - Pure text outperforms text + image
7. **Messy infographic style** - Informal/whiteboard aesthetic signals authenticity, outperforms polished visuals
8. **Tuesday visual =/= Monday topic** - Differentiate to prevent algorithm cannibalization
9. **Specific > abstract** - "Here is the audit clause nobody reads" >> "Data without questions is just numbers"
10. **Reference-worthy content drives saves** - Checklists, frameworks, comparison visuals get bookmarked
11. **Wednesday hook test: confrontational > informational** - Every Wednesday hook must name an adversary, create a knowledge gap, or provoke discomfort. Informational openers ("White bagging saves money," "Data without questions") consistently hit 183-499 impressions. Confrontational openers ("Your PBM contract defines what you pay," "'Traditional' pricing is a polite way of saying...") hit 1,993-41,594. This is the single highest-impact variable.
12. **Friday question format outperforms statement format** - "Who chose your specialty pharmacy?" (2,549 impressions) vs. generic "Here is the [topic]" (500-917). Questions that imply the reader is missing something work best.
13. **Save-to-impression ratio as quality indicator** - Track weekly. High saves relative to low impressions signals educational value even during low-reach weeks (Week 13: ~0.35% save rate on 1,731 impressions).
14. **Newsletter is the primary conversion engine** - 605 subs, 49% open rate, 417 article views (Ed 5). Subscriber-to-follower ratio at 44%. Newsletter metrics are immune to LinkedIn seasonal feed dips.

### Wednesday Hook Checklist (Pre-Publish Gate)
Before publishing any Wednesday text post, verify:
- [ ] Does the hook name or imply an adversary? (PBM, carrier, provider, contract)
- [ ] Does it create a knowledge gap the reader needs to close?
- [ ] Would a CFO reading it feel slightly uncomfortable?
- [ ] Is it confrontational, not informational?

If all four are not checked, rewrite the hook before publishing.

### Posting Time Optimization
- **Newsletters:** 7:30 AM (Substack), 7:45 AM (LinkedIn)
- **Feed Posts:** 8:30 AM EST
- **Company Reshares:** 1:30 PM EST

### How to Diagnose: Seasonal Dip vs. Content Problem

| Signal | Seasonal Dip | Content Problem |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| All posts drop proportionally | Yes | No |
| One post drops, others hold | No | Likely content |
