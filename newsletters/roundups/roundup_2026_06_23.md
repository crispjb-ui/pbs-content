### The PBM Lobby Is Suing to Keep Two States From Reading Its Books

*PLANSPONSOR | June 23, 2026*

PCMA, the trade group for the largest PBMs, filed federal lawsuits to block new pharmacy benefit laws in Illinois and Tennessee, arguing ERISA preempts them for employer-sponsored plans. The Illinois law would force PBMs to report granular pricing data to the state and to their payer clients: what the plan paid the PBM, what the PBM paid the pharmacy, and the size of the manufacturer rebate.

That reporting is exactly the data I want every self-funded client to have. When the industry spends legal dollars fighting disclosure, it is telling you what the disclosure is worth. You do not have to wait for a statute that may sit in court for two years. Ask your broker to write those same transparency and audit rights into your contract now. The lever you control is the renewal, not the legislature.

---

### Indiana Capped What Hospitals Can Charge Employers

*KFF Health News | June 22, 2026*

Indiana enacted a law requiring its five largest nonprofit hospital systems to offer employers direct contracts at or below 260% of Medicare rates by 2029, or risk losing their tax-exempt status. Health policy analysts are watching it as a national bellwether for state price regulation of job-based plans.

This reads as a hospital story, but a large share of specialty pharmacy spend hides inside hospital bills. Infused and provider-administered drugs get marked up far above acquisition cost when they run through a hospital outpatient department. A 260% ceiling is still a markup, but it is a ceiling. The action item is the same one we raise in every claims review: ask your broker where your specialty infusions are being administered, and whether a lower-cost site of care is available.

---

### Eli Lilly Pulled 340B Discounts From Hospitals That Would Not Share Data

*STAT News | June 18, 2026*

Eli Lilly followed through on its threat and began stripping 340B discounted pricing from a few dozen hospitals that did not submit claims data under its new policy. Roughly 70% of covered entities complied. Hospital groups called the move illegal and asked for federal intervention.

I am not rooting for the manufacturer here. But notice what the fight is actually about: visibility into where deeply discounted drugs end up. When a hospital buys a specialty drug at a 340B discount and bills your plan near list price, that spread is the hospital's margin, not your savings. The same opacity that frustrates Lilly is the opacity sitting in your specialty claims. Worth a conversation with your broker about how your plan's 340B-eligible claims are priced.

---

### Medicare Is Testing AI Prior Authorization, and Doctors Are Not Impressed

*KFF Health News | June 23, 2026*

Medicare's WISeR pilot is using artificial intelligence to preapprove certain services. Federal officials say it curbs fraud and contains cost. Physicians and patients in the trial describe delays and errors. Separately, CMS is advancing an electronic prior authorization rule for prescription drugs.

What CMS pilots, commercial plans and PBMs adopt. AI-driven prior authorization can cut administrative friction or it can manufacture denials at scale, and the line between the two lives in how it is configured. For your plan, the question is whether prior authorization is preventing inappropriate spend or simply delaying care and shifting cost back to members. Ask your PBM for approval, denial, and overturn rates on its top PA categories.

The GLP-1 market is shifting again, with new entrants and a next wave of weight-loss agents in the pipeline. None of it changes your formulary this month, but it will shape your 2027 renewal math. Keep it on the radar before budget season, not after.
